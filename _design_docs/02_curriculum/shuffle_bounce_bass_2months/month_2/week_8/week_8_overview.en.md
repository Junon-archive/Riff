# Week 8 — Graduation: Recording a Bounce Groove Melting Swing-16, Ghosts, and Laid-Back
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w8

This is your two-month <mark>graduation piece</mark>. Two months ago even the shuffle felt stiff; now you melt everything you've learned — **swing-16**, **ghosts**, and **laid-back** — into one groove for a sticky bounce that smells of neo-soul and hip-hop. It's a two-bar structure: bar 1 is a **main bounce** of root + 5th + b7 + ghosts, and bar 2 flows into a **melodic variation** with more 5th and b7. Through non-stop rehearsal you leave a final recording, and play it for the you of two months ago. Then we send you off to the next track (**slap and walking bass**). By week's end you'll <mark>finish and record the full bounce groove</mark>. The fingering is identical on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's goal
Combine swing-16, ghosts, and laid-back into one bar, grow it into a two-bar main + variation structure, run a non-stop full run-through, and on the final day record the full bounce groove as a finished piece at 75 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Combine the elements. A draft melting swing-16 + ghosts + laid-back into one groove.
- **Day 2** — Song structure. A two-bar tune with a main bounce + melodic variation.
- **Day 3** — Rehearsal. A non-stop, repeating full run-through.
- **Day 4** — Final graduation recording. Record the full bounce on 4- and 5-string + a send-off to the next track.

## Core feel
- **Melt what you learned into one.** Swing-16, ghosts, and laid-back in one groove.
- **Play through without stopping.** Keep the flow even if you slip.
- **Record the graduation piece.** Next track: slap and walking bass.
