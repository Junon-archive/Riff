# Day 3 — Ghost + Swing-16, A Bounce That Gets Sticky on Boom-Chka

## ① Theory

Today you tuck last week's **ghost ("chka")** into the 16th grid. Fill the empty cells of the root-only line with ghosts and <mark>the root goes "boom," then "chka-chka" backs it up, and the groove gets far stickier.</mark> Yesterday's room, this time filled with sound — and pitchless percussive sound at that.

The layout is simple. Put the **root on the first cell** of each beat and lay **ghosts** on the rest. The ghost is that pitchless "chka" you make by resting the left hand lightly — remember? <mark>Keep the root clear and the ghost a touch softer, and the bounce rolls smoothly.</mark> Add swing-16 to delay the back cell and the stickiness doubles.

Once it's comfortable, add a drop of the **5th**. Slip E's 5th, **B** (3rd string, 2nd fret), between the beats and the line starts to sing. <mark>Wedge one 5th between root and ghost and color returns to a flat groove.</mark> Think of it as a trailer for tomorrow's finished groove.

Learn two lines at **BPM 70**. A bounce that rolls on root and ghost alone, and a bounce with a touch of the 5th on top. <mark>When the boom-chka settles into swing-16 and the 5th adds color, today is a win.</mark> The fingering is the same on 4- or 5-string.

## ③ Practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
Lightly review last week's ghost "chka" alternating with the open E root and loosen up. Check the dry "chka" comes out.

**10–20 min · Brain training**
Retrace slowly whether the root on the first cell and the ghosts on the rest land in place. <mark>Check that boom and chka sit evenly across the four 16th cells.</mark>

**20–40 min · Real play**
Alternate the **root + ghost bounce** and the **bounce with the 5th** at BPM 70. Learn it on the 4-string, then confirm on the 5-string.

**40–50 min · Record**
Record the bounce with the 5th and listen back. Check the ghosts make the stickiness and the 5th adds color.

**Done when:** you can weave root and ghost into the 16th grid with a drop of the 5th to make a swing-16 bounce groove, and confirm it on both 4- and 5-string.

## ④ Tips / Common mistakes

- **Chka covering the boom.** If the ghost is bigger than the root, the bounce gets heavy. Keep the chka a touch softer.
- **Ghost ringing.** Rest the left hand too gently and a "hmmm" hum leaks out. Rest just enough to kill the string.
- **Pressing the 5th too hard.** The 5th is a drop of color. As big as the root, it makes the line heavy.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Cover the unused low B with the thumb through the whole bar.
