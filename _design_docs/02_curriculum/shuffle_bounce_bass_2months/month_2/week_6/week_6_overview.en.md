# Week 6 — Swing-16 Bounce: A Sticky Groove from Long-Short Sixteenths
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w6

If the shuffle was a long-short of eighths, this week's bounce is a <mark>long-short of sixteenths</mark>. Set up a **16th grid** ("1-e-and-a") that splits one beat four ways, then **delay the back sixteenth a touch** and out comes the sticky **swing-16** bounce of neo-soul and hip-hop. Tuck the **ghosts** you learned last week between the notes and the groove comes even more alive. Think of it as nudging only the back slot of sixteen hi-hat cells slightly back. The trick is <mark>the ease of laying the back slot down</mark> without rushing. By week's end you'll finish a swing-16 bounce groove weaving root, 5th, b7, and ghosts. The fingering is identical on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's goal
Count the 16th grid clearly, delay the back sixteenth to make the swing-16 feel, combine the ghosts, and on the final day finish a sticky swing-16 bounce groove at 75 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — The 16th grid. Count "1-e-and-a" and delay the back slot for a swing-16 pulse.
- **Day 2** — Bounce root line. A swing-16 root with a neo-soul scent.
- **Day 3** — Ghost + swing-16. Tuck in ghosts for a stickier bounce.
- **Day 4** — Swing-16 groove. Finish root, 5th, b7, and ghosts on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **Bounce is the long-short of sixteenths.** The shuffle feel, one step finer.
- **Delay the back slot.** Don't rush — lay it slightly back.
- **Sticky with ghosts.** Fill the gaps and the bounce comes alive.
