# Day 4 — The Ghost Groove, Root + Ghost Bounce

## ① Theory

It's the **final day** of the week. <mark>On Monday even one "chka" felt foreign; today you finish a groove where root and ghost alternate and bounce.</mark> The dead note, the boom-chka, and the room are all in your hands now. All that's left is to tie the three into one groove and capture it as sound.

Today's piece is the **root + ghost bounce**. Boom the open E root as an eighth, chka the 3rd-string ghost as an eighth, and roll them alternating through the whole bar. <mark>When boom-chka-boom-chka runs on without a break, the groove keeps bouncing forward.</mark> Every feel you learned this week is packed into this one bar.

The key is a **steady flow**. The eight eighths have to be even for the bounce to stay smooth. Boom clear, chka a touch softer, <mark>unplayed strings quiet</mark> — keep just those three and the groove comes alive. Turn one bar over without a wobble at **BPM 80**.

The recording just needs to be **an honest take, not a perfect one**. <mark>Play Monday's single "chka" beside today's groove, and a week's growth rings out clearly.</mark> That sound is Week 5's diploma. On a **5-string** finish it the same way while resting the low B.

## ③ Practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
Lightly review this week's boom-chka and room at BPM 70 to loosen up. Check the dry "chka" comes out well.

**10–20 min · Brain training**
Retrace very slowly whether the ghost lands on its exact spot. Check <mark>whether boom and chka split evenly as eighths.</mark>

**20–40 min · Real play (the Week 5 piece)**
Repeat the **root + ghost bounce** at **BPM 80**. The goal is to keep turning one bar over without a wobble. Learn it on the 4-string, then confirm on the 5-string.

**40–50 min · Record (the graduation take)**
Record the root + ghost bounce. Listen with Monday's single "chka" side by side, and keep this week's finish.

**Done when:** you can finish a bouncing groove alternating root and ghost at BPM 80, and keep a recording on both 4- and 5-string. (Week 5 complete!)

## ④ Tips / Common mistakes

- **Chka covering the boom.** If the ghost is bigger than the root, the bounce gets heavy. Keep the chka a touch softer.
- **Uneven eighths.** If the boom-chka gaps spread, the bounce disappears. Line up all eight exactly to the metronome.
- **Rushing the tempo.** A wobbly BPM 80 is no use. Even first, slow if needed.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Cover the unused low B with the thumb through the whole bar.
