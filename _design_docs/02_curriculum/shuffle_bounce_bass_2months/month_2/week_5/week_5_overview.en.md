# Week 5 — Make It Bounce with Ghosts: Starting the Bounce with a Pitchless "Chka"
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w5

Now that last month's shuffle is behind you, this week opens the world of bounce. The secret to a bouncing groove isn't the notes — it's the **ghost** ("chka"). Rest your left hand lightly on the string to make a pitchless **dead note**, drop it between the roots, and the groove starts to breathe and bounce. Think of it like a drum's hi-hat filler. The trick is making the ghost a <mark>clean "chka"</mark> and quietly killing the strings you don't play with **muting**. By week's end you'll alternate root and ghost to finish a <mark>groove with bounce</mark>. The fingering is identical on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's goal
Rest your hand to make a clean pitchless dead note, alternate root and ghost, make space with muting, and on the final day finish a bouncing root + ghost groove at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Dead note "chka". Rest your left hand lightly for one clean, pitchless ghost.
- **Day 2** — Root + ghost. Alternate boom (root) and chka (ghost) into a groove.
- **Day 3** — Make space. Kill the unplayed strings with muting for room in the groove.
- **Day 4** — Ghost groove. Finish the root + ghost bounce on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **A ghost isn't a note.** Rest your left hand lightly for just a "chka".
- **Kill the unplayed strings.** The ghost comes alive only when it's quiet.
- **The filler makes it bounce.** Fill the gaps and the groove breathes.
