# Day 3 — Taste a Half-Time Shuffle, A Wide, Spreading Feel

## ① Theory

Today you taste a <mark>wide, spreading feel</mark> — the **half-time shuffle**. Half-time is the sense that, at the same tempo, the beat feels wide, as if at **half speed**. Instead of the drum snare coming densely on 2 and 4, it comes big every other beat, and the tune widens right out. At the very same tempo, the tune turns twice as easy — that's the magic.

The bass holds this wide space with a **big root**. Instead of filling densely, it steps heavily on **beats 1 and 3** and rolls only lightly between them with swing-16 ghosts. <mark>The wider the space, the more the half-time ease comes alive.</mark>

Lay this week's **laid-back** on top and it's the icing on the cake. Lay the wide beat slightly back, and the shuffle's stickiness and the half-time's width are felt at once. <mark>The ease of not rushing is again today's core.</mark> Set down the urge to fill in a hurry. Holding back the hand that wants to fill is today's practice too.

Today is a "taste." Rather than mastering it, the goal is to experience the **wide feel** in your body. At **BPM 72**, build the skeleton first, then lay ghosts on. Don't fear the wide space — the trick is to enjoy that empty room instead. <mark>If one bar rolls wide and easy, today is a success.</mark> The fingering is identical on 4- or 5-string.

## ③ Practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
Loosen up laying the open E root big on beats 1 and 3. Find the feel of leaving wide space first.

**10–20 min · Brain training**
Count how wide the gap is between the beat 1 and beat 3 roots. <mark>Slowly set down the urge to fill the empty cells in a hurry.</mark>

**20–40 min · Real play**
Build the **half-time shuffle** from the skeleton at **BPM 72**, then lay ghosts on and repeat. Learn it on the 4-string, then confirm on the 5-string.

**40–50 min · Record**
Record the half-time skeleton and the ghost version separately and listen side by side. Check by ear that it's wide and easy.

**Done when:** you can step big on the root at beats 1 and 3, lay swing-16 ghosts between, and feel the wide half-time shuffle on both 4- and 5-string.

## ④ Tips / Common mistakes

- **Rushing to fill the empty cells.** The half-time width comes from space. Add more notes and the width vanishes.
- **Ghosts too strong.** A ghost is a dry "chka." As big as the root, it blurs the skeleton.
- **Slowing down because it's wide.** Half-time isn't slow, it's wide. Keep the tempo as is.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Cover the unused low B with the thumb.
