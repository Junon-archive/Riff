# Day 1 — Lie Behind the Beat, Behind-the-Beat Laid-Back Root

## ① Theory

Last week you got the swing-16 bounce into your hands. This week you add a **subtle layer of timing** on top. Pocket masters don't nail the beat dead-on — they <mark>lie just behind it</mark>, which is called **laid-back** (behind the beat). It's like the ease of settling back into a sofa just behind the beat.

Today you catch that feel with **a single root**. When the metronome clicks, instead of landing right on top of it, you place the note **a touch late**. <mark>The tempo doesn't slow down — only the placement moves back.</mark> Lean back without rushing and the groove gains ease and weight. It's like taking a step back behind the drummer and waiting at ease.

The trick is to **lie back without slowing down**. Lying behind the beat and missing the beat are completely different. The metronome stays on the grid, and only your root sits softly behind it. <mark>Not fearing the rests is also today's core</mark> — the more empty cells there are, the better you feel the ease of lying back.

**BPM 72** is plenty. Record a root right on the grid and a root laid slightly back, then listen to them side by side. <mark>If you can hear the difference between the two, today is a success.</mark> The hands do the same thing on 4- or 5-string, so start with whichever is comfortable.

## ③ Practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
Loosen up by lightly laying the right-hand two fingers on the open E. Drop the tension and find the feel of plucking a single root comfortably first.

**10–20 min · Brain training**
Turn on the metronome, nail the root on the grid, then push it back just a touch. <mark>Check by ear that the tempo stays put and only the placement moves back.</mark>

**20–40 min · Real play**
Repeat the **laid-back root** at **BPM 72**. Build the beat with the steady pulse, then practice the root lying back with space. Learn it on the 4-string, then confirm on the 5-string.

**40–50 min · Record**
Record the on-grid root and the laid-back root separately and listen side by side. Check by ear whether it lies back and stays unhurried.

**Done when:** at metronome 72 you can lay the root slightly behind the beat, tell the on-grid root from the laid-back root by ear, and confirm it on both 4- and 5-string.

## ④ Tips / Common mistakes

- **Slowing down as you lie back.** Laid-back only moves the placement back. Slow the tempo too and it's just slow playing. Keep the metronome on the grid.
- **Lying back too far.** A touch is enough. Too far back and it sounds like you missed the beat.
- **Rushing to fill the empty cells.** The space is the ease of laid-back. Resist the urge to add more notes.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Cover the unused low B with the thumb.
