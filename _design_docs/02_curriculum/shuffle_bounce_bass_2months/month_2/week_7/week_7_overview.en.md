# Week 7 — Laid-Back & Pocket: The Ease of Lying Behind the Beat
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w7

This week you learn a subtle layer of timing. Pocket masters don't nail the beat dead-on — they <mark>lie just behind it</mark>, which is called **laid-back** (behind the beat). Lean back without rushing and the groove gains ease and weight. Push and pull the same line across three positions — **ahead, on, and behind** — and feel the difference in your body. You'll also taste a **half-time shuffle** for a wide, spreading feel. The trick is <mark>the ease of not rushing</mark>, unafraid of rests and space. By week's end you'll finish a spacious laid-back bounce groove. The fingering is identical on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's goal
Learn the feel of lying behind the beat, contrast the same line across ahead, on, and behind, taste a half-time shuffle, and on the final day finish a spacious laid-back bounce groove at 72 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Lie behind the beat. Place the root slightly late with a behind-the-beat feel.
- **Day 2** — Push-pull, three positions. Put the same line ahead, on, and behind to compare.
- **Day 3** — Taste a half-time shuffle. A wide feel with snare on 2 and 4 and a swing-16 lilt.
- **Day 4** — Laid-back groove. Finish a spacious swing-16 bounce on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **Lie back without slowing down.** Keep the tempo — only the placement moves back.
- **Don't fear the rests.** Space makes the ease.
- **Don't rush to fill.** The empty room is the groove's weight.
