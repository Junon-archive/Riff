# Month 2 — Bounce: Swinging the Sixteenths and Filling with Ghosts to Record a Sticky Groove in 30 Days
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 2

This month in one line: **the goal of your second month is one step past the shuffle — getting the bounce that swings the sixteenths.** First you fill between roots with pitchless **ghosts** ("chka") to make the groove bounce, then make a neo-soul / hip-hop bounce with **swing-16**, delaying the back slot of a **16th grid** split four ways per beat. You add **laid-back** feel — lying behind the beat — plus the wide feel of a half-time shuffle, and finally melt everything you've learned into one to finish and record a <mark>sticky bounce groove</mark>. You learn 4- and 5-string together, so any bass is fine — fifty minutes a day, and the measure isn't speed, it's <mark>the taste of a bounce that sways on its own</mark>.

## This month's journey
- **Week 5** — Make it bounce with ghosts. Fill between roots with pitchless "chka" for a breathing groove.
- **Week 6** — Swing-16 bounce. Delay the back slot of the sixteenths for a neo-soul / hip-hop bounce.
- **Week 7** — Laid-back & pocket. Lie behind the beat and taste a half-time shuffle.
- **Week 8** — Graduate. Melt swing-16, ghosts, and laid-back into one groove and record.

## What changes by the end
Eight weeks ago you only knew straight time; now you melt swing-16, ghosts, and laid-back into one groove and record a <mark>sticky bounce piece</mark> from start to finish. Your two-month graduation piece stays in your hands, and you're ready to move on to the next track (slap and walking bass).
