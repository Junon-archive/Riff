# Week 1 — Feel the Triangle: Rolling Eighths Long-Short for Your First Shuffle Root
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

There's no boogie or chord move yet this week. Instead, you start by <mark>feeling the beat as a triangle</mark>. Counting a **triplet** ("1-and-a") that splits one beat into three, you roll the once-even eighth notes **long-short** (da-ga) to turn them into **swing**. On top, you lay an **E root** and play it as a crisp shuffle in front of the metronome. Think of the da-ga rhythm of a galloping horse's hooves. By week's end, you'll have your <mark>very first rolling shuffle root pulse</mark>. Whether it's a 4- or 5-string, the strings are the same, so any bass can follow right along.

## This week's goal
Count the triplet grid clearly, turn straight eighths into long-short swing, lay an E root on top, and on the final day roll a shuffle root pulse evenly at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — The triplet grid. Count "1-and-a" to feel one beat as three, and put a root over the triplet.
- **Day 2** — Straight to swing. Drop the middle note to roll long-short (da-ga) for swung eighths.
- **Day 3** — Shuffle root pulse. Play an E root evenly as a shuffle against the metronome.
- **Day 4** — Shuffle root over two chords. Finish the long-short shuffle root pulse on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **Forget speed.** The only measure is a rolling long-short.
- **The beat is a triangle.** Feel one beat as three and swing appears.
- **Front long, back short.** Land on the first and third slots of the triplet.
