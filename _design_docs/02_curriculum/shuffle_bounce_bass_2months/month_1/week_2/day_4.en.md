# Day 4 — E Boogie Shuffle Groove, Week 2 Complete

## ① Theory

It's the final day of Week 2. The **R-5-6-b7** boogie walk you built all week — today you <mark>finish it into one groove.</mark> Roll the ascent and descent as a long-short shuffle and the bouncing **E boogie shuffle bassline** finally lands in your hands. It's this week's finish line.

The finished piece is two measures. **Measure 1 is the ascent** (R-5-6-b7), **measure 2 is the descent** (b7-6-5-R) — exactly the round trip you learned yesterday. Only one thing changed: <mark>now you lay a firm long-short shuffle feel and roll it bouncing.</mark> What you stepped cleanly up the stairs, today you roll into a "groove."

With your **two fingers**, roll the front of each note a touch long and the back short. **Metronome 80** is this week's target tempo, but <mark>the bounce comes before the number.</mark> Bouncing at 70 beats stiff at 80 by far. Bring the roll alive at a comfortable speed, then raise it slowly.

This boogie shape moves to **any chord** unchanged. Today you finish it in the **key of E**, but change only the root and the same shape works in A or D — you already hold the backbone of a whole shuffle blues. On a **5-string** the fingering is the same; test a heavier version with the low **B** root too. Finish this week with a smile too — <mark>a single staircase has become a song.</mark>

## ③ Practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
Roll yesterday's **boogie round trip** on open E at BPM 60 to loosen up. Check the up-and-down connects before you start.

**10–20 min · Brain training**
Retrace the **E boogie shuffle groove** very slowly, ascent and descent shape. Check <mark>whether the stairs hold steady even as you add the long-short</mark>.

**20–40 min · Real play (this week's piece)**
Repeat the pinned **E boogie shuffle groove** at **BPM 80**. The goal is a bouncing long-short that won't wobble — the bounce comes first. Learn it on the 4-string, then confirm the same groove on the 5-string.

**40–50 min · Record/feedback**
Record 30 seconds and listen for whether **the boogie bounces at BPM 80**. Note the BPM you reached this week.

**Done when:** you can roll the E boogie shuffle groove (the R-5-6-b7 round trip) at BPM 80 with a bouncing long-short, on both a 4-string and a 5-string. (Week 2 complete!)

## ④ Tips / Common mistakes

- **The long-short goes flat.** With four notes, it's easy to lose the shuffle feel. Keep the front of each note firmly long.
- **It stiffens at 80.** Raising the tempo makes the bounce vanish. Keep the bouncing feel from 70 and raise it slowly.
- **The round-trip seam breaks.** The turn at the top b7 comes late. Prepare the turn early and cross smoothly.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Keep B deadened with the thumb even as the groove gets busy.
