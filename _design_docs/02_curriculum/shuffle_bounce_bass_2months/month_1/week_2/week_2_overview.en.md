# Week 2 — The Boogie Walk: A Bouncing Shuffle Bassline with R-5-6-b7
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

Last week you rolled a shuffle root; this week you stack notes on that root to make a <mark>bouncing boogie bass</mark>. The key is the **boogie walk** — a move that climbs and descends from the root through the **5th**, **6th**, and **b7** like stairs. This walk, up and down with **R-5-6-b7**, becomes the backbone of the shuffle blues. Once you learn the shape by eye on the fretboard — open E to the 5th B, 6th C#, b7 D — it's easy anywhere, because the shape is the same in every chord. By week's end you'll finish an <mark>E boogie shuffle groove</mark> round trip. The fingering is identical on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's goal
Stack a 5th behind the root, climb the boogie with R-5-6-b7, run the ascent and descent as a round trip, and on the final day finish a bouncing E boogie shuffle groove at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Root + 5th. Put a 5th on the swung back note ("a") for an R-5 shuffle.
- **Day 2** — Boogie ascent. Climb the stairs with R-5-6-b7 and burn the fretboard shape in.
- **Day 3** — Boogie round trip. Ascend, then descend up and down the walk.
- **Day 4** — Boogie shuffle groove. Finish the E boogie ascent and descent on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **Bounce up and down the stairs.** R-5-6-b7 is the skeleton of the boogie.
- **The same shape moves.** Memorize the shape and any chord change stays easy.
- **Keep the long-short.** Even with more notes, don't lose the shuffle feel.
