# Week 4 — Tying It Off with a Turnaround: A Full 12-Bar Shuffle Blues (Month 1 Graduation)
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w4

A month ago the shuffle felt foreign; this week you tie off the 12 bars yourself to <mark>play a full shuffle blues</mark>. The key is the **turnaround** — the last bars of the 12 that loop the tune back to the top. Move the boogie shape through **V(B7)-IV(A7)-I(E7)-V(B7)**, and instead of ending, one lap flows naturally into the next. Add a touch of **dynamics** and fill for expression, and the shuffle stops being flat. By week's end you'll <mark>play the 12-bar shuffle blues from start to finish without stopping and record it</mark>. The fingering is identical on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's goal
Tie off the last four bars with a V-IV-I-V turnaround, add expression with dynamics, rehearse the 12 bars without stopping, and on the final day play and record a full 12-bar shuffle blues at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Turnaround. The last four bars that loop the tune back with V-IV-I-V.
- **Day 2** — Dynamics and fill. Add a little variation for an expressive shuffle.
- **Day 3** — Rehearsal. Run all 12 bars at once without stopping.
- **Day 4** — Play through and record. Record the 12-bar shuffle blues on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **The turnaround loops the tune.** The last four bars lead into the next lap.
- **Play through without stopping.** Keep the flow even if you slip.
- **Record the finished piece.** Carve a month of shuffle into your hands.
