# Day 3 — Rehearsal, All 12 Bars Without Stopping

## ① Theory

Now all the pieces are gathered. The E and A boogies, the turnaround, the small fill — <mark>the stairs you stacked one by one over a month connect into a single line today.</mark> There's nothing new to learn today. Instead it's a rehearsal that runs **all 12 bars without stopping**, in one go.

Let me lay out the 12-bar form again. **| E7 A7 E7 E7 | A7 A7 E7 E7 | B7 A7 E7 B7 |** — the first eight bars move between I and IV, and <mark>the last four bars are the turnaround you drilled up to yesterday.</mark> Once this order pictures itself in your mind, your hands already know each spot.

The heart of a rehearsal is **flow, not perfection**. <mark>Even if you miss a note in the middle, don't stop — move on to the next bar.</mark> On stage, a stop sounds louder than a slip. Pass by the wrong spot and keep the flow — that's today's real goal.

The target tempo is **BPM 80**, but don't get tied to the number. A lap that turns smoothly at 70 beats one that breaks at 80. <mark>Keep the flow alive and the speed follows on its own.</mark> On a **5-string** the fingering is the same; just rest the low B. Tomorrow you'll capture this lap as a recording.

▶ **5-string.** Same notes and spots as the 4-string. Cover the low **B** with the thumb so it doesn't leak through the whole lap.

## ③ Practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
Roll the **three boogies** in E·A·B order at BPM 60 to loosen up. Check all three spots come out.

**10–20 min · Brain training**
Retrace the **four bars of the turnaround** very slowly. Pre-drill <mark>the last row, which tangles most often.</mark>

**20–40 min · Real play (this week's rehearsal)**
Repeat **all 12 bars** at **BPM 80** without stopping. The goal is to keep the flow even if you slip. Learn it on the 4-string, then confirm on the 5-string.

**40–50 min · Record/feedback**
Record 30 seconds and listen for whether **the 12 bars stay unbroken at BPM 80**. Note the BPM you reached today.

**Done when:** you can pass through the 12-bar shuffle blues from start to finish at BPM 80 without stopping, one lap on both a 4-string and a 5-string.

## ④ Tips / Common mistakes

- **Hesitating at bar 9.** The moment you cross into the turnaround is the most dangerous. Prepare the end of bar 8 ahead of time.
- **It breaks at 80.** Raising the tempo makes the change come late. Turn smoothly at 70 first, then raise it slowly.
- **Stopping when you slip.** Stopping over one wrong note collapses the whole flow. Pass by the wrong spot and move to the next bar.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Keep the unused low string covered with the thumb through the whole lap.
