# Week 3 — The 12-Bar Shuffle: Moving the Boogie Shape to I·IV·V for One Lap
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

Now that the boogie shape is in your hands, this week you move it around to go <mark>once around 12 bars</mark>. In blues, **12 bars** make one lap. At the three spots — **I(E7)-IV(A7)-V(B7)** — you finish the tune just by **moving the same boogie shape** you memorized last week. For IV you shift to the A root (open 3rd string), for V to the B root, laying the same shape in a new place. The trick is crossing the moment a chord changes **smoothly**. By week's end, moving the boogie shape at each chord, you'll <mark>pass through the 12-bar shuffle without stopping</mark>. The fingering is identical on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's goal
Move the boogie shape to IV(A7) and V(B7), connect the chord changes smoothly, cycle through the 12 bars in order, and on the final day pass through the 12-bar shuffle without stopping at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Move to IV. Shift the boogie shape to A7 (A root = open 3rd string) for an A boogie.
- **Day 2** — Move to V. Shift the same shape to the B7 spot for a B boogie.
- **Day 3** — Cycle 12 bars. Change chords smoothly in I-IV-V order.
- **Day 4** — 12-bar shuffle line. Finish the I→IV move on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **Same shape, new place.** Don't change the shape — just the position.
- **Prepare the change early.** Look at the next chord's root one beat ahead.
- **12 bars is one lap.** Memorize the order in your body and it won't break.
