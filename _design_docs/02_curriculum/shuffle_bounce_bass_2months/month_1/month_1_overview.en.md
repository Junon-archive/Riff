# Month 1 — Shuffle: Rolling Eighths Long-Short to Play a Full 12-Bar Shuffle Blues in 30 Days
> Curriculum: shuffle_bounce_bass_2months · Month 1

This month in one line: **the goal of your first month isn't flashy speed — it's getting the shuffle, rolling eighth notes long-short (da-ga).** First you count the **triplet** (the triangle) to turn straight into swing, then lay an **E root** over it as a shuffle. Next you build a bouncing bassline with a **boogie walk** (R-5-6-b7), and move that shape to the **I(E7)-IV(A7)-V(B7)** spots to go once around 12 bars. Finally you tie it off with a **turnaround** to play a full <mark>12-bar shuffle blues</mark> and record it. You learn 4- and 5-string together, so any bass is fine — fifty minutes a day, and the measure isn't speed, it's <mark>a groove that rolls on its own</mark>.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Feel the triangle. Count the triplet, turn straight into swing, and lay an E root shuffle on top.
- **Week 2** — Build the boogie. Stack the 5th, 6th, and b7 for a bouncing R-5-6-b7 bassline.
- **Week 3** — Once around 12 bars. Move the boogie shape to I(E7)-IV(A7)-V(B7).
- **Week 4** — Finish + graduate. Tie it off with a turnaround and record a full 12-bar shuffle blues.

## What changes by the end
Four weeks ago the shuffle felt foreign; now you play a 12-bar shuffle blues from start to finish without stopping. The triplet, boogie walk, and turnaround stay in your hands, and you reach the goal — a <mark>bouncing shuffle groove</mark>. In Month 2 (bounce) you'll swing the sixteenths and add ghosts and laid-back feel, ready to move on to a <mark>sticky bounce groove</mark>.
