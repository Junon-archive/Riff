# Week 4 — Swing Quarter Notes and a 12-Bar F Blues: Your First Full Walk
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w4

At last the skeleton takes the stage. You weave chord tones and approach notes in <mark>swing quarter notes</mark> to walk a **12-bar F blues** (F7-Bb7-C7) from start to finish. **Beat 1 lands, beat 4 bridges** — once this rhythm settles into your body, that's <mark>real walking</mark>. A month ago you fingered just one root note; by the end of this week you walk 12 bars in swing all the way through, and even record it. This week is your **M1 graduation**. You complete the walk with the same line on both **4-string** and **5-string**.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you walk a **12-bar F blues** in swing quarter notes without stopping, and beat-1 landings and beat-4 bridges carry on steadily. On the final day you record an <mark>F blues 12-bar walk</mark> on 4- and 5-string and graduate M1.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — The swing quarter-note feel. An even, forward-moving step.
- **Day 2** — First half of the 12 bars (the F7-Bb7 section). Get through 8 bars.
- **Day 3** — Second half (including the C7 turnaround) + rehearsal. A full run-through.
- **Day 4** — Walk and record the 12-bar F blues (M1 graduation, 4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **Swing moves forward.** Keep the quarter notes ahead, never dragging back.
- **Beat 1 lands, beat 4 bridges.** Hold this rhythm through all 12 bars.
- **Don't stop.** Even if you slip, keep walking without breaking the flow.
