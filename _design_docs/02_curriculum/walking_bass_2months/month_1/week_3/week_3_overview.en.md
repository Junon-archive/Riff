# Week 3 — Approach Notes: Building a Bridge to the Next Chord
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

Walking's real secret is <mark>the approach note on the last beat (beat 4)</mark>. An approach note is like a stepping stone across to the next stone: put a note a **half step above or below** the next chord's root on beat 4, and you <mark>land as if sliding</mark> into beat 1 of the next measure. There are three kinds of approach — **chromatic (half step), scale, and dominant** — and the bass always **aims at the next chord in advance**. This week you build a bridge on beat 4 to connect chord to chord. **4-string** and **5-string** are the same hand, and **5-string** extends into low approach notes too.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you place **an approach note heading to the next chord's root on beat 4** of each measure, so measure connects to measure without a break. You can choose chromatic, scale, and dominant approaches, and on the final day you complete an <mark>F7↔Bb7 approach-note walk</mark> on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Chromatic approach (from a half step above/below into the root). A half-step bridge.
- **Day 2** — Scale and dominant approaches. The three kinds of approach.
- **Day 3** — Put the beat-4 approach note into the line. Walking with approaches.
- **Day 4** — Complete the F7↔Bb7 approach-note walk (4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **Beat 4 is the bridge.** Aim at the next chord with your last step.
- **The half step is strongest.** A half-step approach slides anywhere.
- **Aim ahead.** Picture the next chord's root before you step.
