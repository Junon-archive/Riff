# Month 1 — Walking's Skeleton: From Chord Tones to a 12-Bar F Blues
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 1

This month in one line: **the goal of your first month isn't flashy lines — it's building the skeleton that walks over chords.** Walking is a step that **lands on a chord tone every beat 1** and **builds a bridge to the next chord on the last beat**. First you find the chord tones (**R-3-5-b7**) of **F7, Bb7, and C7** on the fretboard, then raise the density one stage at a time: **root (whole note) → root-5th (half notes) → chord-tone quarter notes**. You add **chromatic approach notes** to connect the chords, and finally <mark>walk a full 12-bar F blues in swing quarter notes</mark> and record it. You learn **4-string** and **5-string** together, so any bass is fine — fifty minutes a day, and the measure isn't speed, it's <mark>a step that lands crisply</mark>.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Find the chord tones. Locate R-3-5-b7 of F7, Bb7, and C7 on the fretboard to gather your walking material.
- **Week 2** — From skeleton to line. Raise the density one stage at a time: root → root-5th → chord-tone quarter notes.
- **Week 3** — Approach notes. Use chromatic, scale, and dominant approaches to bridge to the next chord on beat 4.
- **Week 4** — Swing quarter notes + a full 12-bar F blues, recorded (M1 graduation).

## What changes by the end
A month ago you fingered just the root; now you weave **chord tones** and **approach notes** to <mark>walk a full 12-bar F blues from start to finish</mark>. The skeleton you built with the **density ramp** stays in your hands, and the rhythm of landing on beat 1 and bridging on beat 4 settles into your body. Next month (navigating changes) you move on to **ii-V-I**, **enclosure**, and ornaments — ready to grow into <mark>real jazz walking</mark>.
