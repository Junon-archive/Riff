# Week 2 — From Skeleton to Line: Density One Stage at a Time
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

Walking doesn't fill everything from the start. Just as you stand before you walk and sit before you stand, you raise <mark>the density one stage at a time</mark>. Fill in the notes in order — **root only (whole note) → root-5th (half notes) → chord-tone quarter notes** — and you'll never collapse from rushing into something flashy. The skeleton has to be solid before a line can stand on it. This week you build <mark>the real skeleton of walking</mark> in quarter notes using chord tones as material, and by the end you reach an arpeggio walk spanning two chords, **F7 → Bb7**. It's the same hand on **4-string** and **5-string**.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you can walk one chord raising its density **root → root-5th → chord-tone quarter notes**, and you land **beat 1 on the root every measure**. On the final day you complete an <mark>F7 → Bb7 arpeggio walk</mark> on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Follow the progression with the root only (whole notes). The bone of the skeleton.
- **Day 2** — Root + 5th (half notes). One stage up to an R-5 line.
- **Day 3** — Chord-tone arpeggio (quarter notes). The feel of a quarter-note walk.
- **Day 4** — Complete the F7 → Bb7 arpeggio walk (4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **The more rushed, the more you start from the skeleton.** Flash goes on last.
- **Beat 1 is the root.** The first step of every measure lands home on the root.
- **One stage at a time.** Whole → half → quarter, no hurrying.
