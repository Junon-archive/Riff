# Week 1 — Find the Chord Tones: The Safe Notes You Can Step On
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

Walking's first step isn't a flashy line — it's knowing <mark>the notes you can step on (chord tones)</mark>. Chord tones are like safe stones you can step on while walking, so once you find each chord's **root, 3rd, 5th, and 7th** on the fretboard, you can walk freely over it. This week you get the chord tones (**R-3-5-b7**) of three dominant chords — **F7, Bb7, and C7** — into your hands. It's fine not to build a line yet; drawing <mark>a map of the chord tones on the fretboard</mark> is all this week is about. The fingering is the same on **4-string** or **5-string**, so just follow right along.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you can finger each **R-3-5-b7** of **F7, Bb7, and C7** on the fretboard without hesitation, and climb up and down those four notes crisply as a **quarter-note arpeggio**. On the final day you complete an <mark>F7 chord-tone arpeggio</mark> on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — F7 chord tones. Etch the R-3-5-b7 positions into the fretboard and play an F7 arpeggio.
- **Day 2** — Bb7 and C7 chord tones. Get all three chords' tones into your hands.
- **Day 3** — Climb up and down the chord tones in quarter notes. The feel of an arpeggio walk.
- **Day 4** — Complete the F7 chord-tone line (4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **Chord tones are safe stones.** Learn the notes you can step on first.
- **The root is home.** Know where to return to the root every time.
- **Speed comes later.** For now, fingering the positions accurately is everything.
