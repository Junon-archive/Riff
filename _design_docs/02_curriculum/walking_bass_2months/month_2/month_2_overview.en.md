# Month 2 — Navigating Changes: From ii-V-I to a Graduation Progression
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 2

This month in one line: **the goal of your second month is to navigate a progression of changing chords smoothly.** You start by walking **ii-V-I**, jazz's most common path (in the key of F, **Gm7-C7-Fmaj7**). You make your landings strong with **enclosure**, narrowing in on a target note from above and below, and breathe life into quarter-note lines with **ghost notes**, **eighth-note fills**, and **hammer-ons**. Finally you <mark>complete walking over an original ii-V-turnaround progression</mark>, record it, and graduate the track. This track is the **bass jazz/harmony capstone**, and you handle **4-string** and **5-string** together to build <mark>a hand that walks the changes</mark> on any bass.

## This month's journey
- **Week 5** — ii-V-I walking. Connect Gm7-C7-Fmaj7 smoothly with approach notes.
- **Week 6** — Enclosure and deeper targeting. Narrow in on a target from above and below for a strong landing.
- **Week 7** — Ornaments and fills. Ghosts, dead notes, eighths, and hammers bring the line to life.
- **Week 8** — Graduation. Complete and record walking over an original ii-V-turnaround progression (track graduation).

## What changes by the end
Two months ago you fingered only the root; now you marshal **chord tones**, **approach notes**, **enclosure**, and **ornaments** to <mark>navigate over the changes smoothly</mark>. A 12-bar F blues and **ii-V-I** walking stay in your hands, and you compose your own <mark>real jazz walking line</mark> — the goal you set out for. Graduating this track means you've grasped the bass's **jazz/harmony capstone**, becoming a bass that walks the changes in any session.
