# Week 6 — Enclosure: Narrowing In on a Target from Above and Below
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w6

Advanced walking approaches a target note by <mark>wrapping around it from above and below</mark>. Enclosure is a technique that lands by narrowing in on the target from both sides — wrap the target **C** with **Db** a half step above and **B** a half step below, then **land on C**, and a tension that <mark>pulls inevitably</mark> into the next chord is born. Where last week's approach was a single note, enclosure squeezes with two notes, above and below. The target can be the **root** or the **3rd**. It's the same hand on **4-string** and **5-string**.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you land by **enclosing** a target with a half step above and below, and your landings grow much stronger. You can enclose whether the target is the **root** or the **3rd**, and on the final day you complete a <mark>chromatic enclosure</mark> on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — The idea of a half-step-above-and-below enclosure. One enclosed landing.
- **Day 2** — Enclose a root target. A root enclosure.
- **Day 3** — Enclose a 3rd target. A 3rd enclosure.
- **Day 4** — Complete the enclosure walk (4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **Squeeze from above and below.** Narrow in on the target with two notes and land.
- **Tension makes the landing strong.** The tighter you squeeze, the clearer the arrival.
- **Choose the target first.** Decide root or 3rd, then enclose it.
