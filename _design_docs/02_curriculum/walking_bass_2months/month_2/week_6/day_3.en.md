# Day 3 — Third enclosure, wrapping the color of the chord and landing

## ① 이론/설명

Yesterday you enclosed the root. Today you move the enclosure target to the chord's **third**. The third is the **color note** that decides whether the chord is bright (major) or dark (minor). <mark>If the root is the home address, the third is the face of that home</mark>. When you enclose the face, the chord's color comes alive most vividly.

The third of the home chord **Fmaj7** in the key of F is **A**. Let's enclose this A — pass through the half-step above, **Bb**, and the half-step below, **G#**, then **land on A**. When you wrap and settle onto the third, you hear a <mark>bright, clear color</mark> unlike landing on the root.

The order is always the same — **above → below → land**. The third is the most beloved enclosure target in jazz walking. Aiming only at the root makes the walk monotonous, but <mark>wrapping and landing on the third gives the line an expression</mark>. Today you complete a wrap-and-land with the third as your target.

## ④ 팁 / 흔한 실수

- **You press the third heavily, like a root.** The third is a color-giving note, so hitting it too hard makes it sound like a root. Keep the landing clear but a touch lighter than the root, and press it as if singing.
- **You press the lower neighbor G# vaguely.** G# (4th string, fret 4) is a leading tone that rises a half-step into A. You must press it precisely for the pull upward to come alive.

Keep today's wrapped third, the single point A, in your eye. If the root is the home address, this third is the chord's expression.
