# Week 8 — Graduation: Walking Over an Original ii-V-Turnaround
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w8

This is your two-month <mark>graduation piece</mark>. Over an original progression that cycles by adding a **turnaround** to **Gm7-C7-Fmaj7**, you marshal chord tones, approach notes, enclosure, and ornaments to <mark>walk smoothly</mark>. Two months ago you fingered only the root; now, when four bars end, you make a line of your own that flows naturally into the first bar of the next cycle. This track is the **bass jazz/harmony capstone**, and this week is its finale. You complete the full walk and record it on both **4-string** and **5-string**.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you walk an original progression that cycles through **ii-V-I + a turnaround** without stopping, packing two months of vocabulary into one line. On the final day you record your <mark>graduation walk</mark> on 4- and 5-string and graduate the walking bass track.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Grasp the progression (ii-V-I + turnaround cycling). Understand the progression.
- **Day 2** — A walking line over the progression (chord tones + approach). A 4-bar walk.
- **Day 3** — Lay on ornaments + rehearsal (cycling without stopping). A full run-through.
- **Day 4** — The final graduation recording (4-bar cycle, 4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **Marshal everything.** Bring out all of two months' vocabulary.
- **The cycle is key.** The end of four bars flows smoothly into the first bar.
- **Don't stop.** For the graduation recording, walk all the way through without breaking the flow.
