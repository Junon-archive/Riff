# Week 5 — ii-V-I Walking: The Road Home in Jazz
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w5

Your second month opens on jazz's most common path, <mark>ii-V-I</mark>. In the key of F that road is **Gm7 → C7 → Fmaj7**: ii opens the door, V builds the tension, and I arrives home — <mark>a journey home</mark>. Last month's chord tones and approach notes are exactly your material — **land on the root** of each chord and connect with a beat-4 **approach note**, and the three chords flow naturally. The only new thing is that **Gm7 is a minor 7**, so you use its **b3**. It's the same hand on **4-string** and **5-string**.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you connect **Gm7-C7-Fmaj7** smoothly, landing on each chord's root, and the **V→I resolution** (C7→Fmaj7) rings clearly in your ear. On the final day you complete an <mark>ii-V-I walk</mark> on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Gm7, C7, and Fmaj7 chord tones. Get the three chords' tones into your hands.
- **Day 2** — Connect Gm7→C7 (approach note). ii→V.
- **Day 3** — Connect C7→Fmaj7. The V→I resolution.
- **Day 4** — Complete the ii-V-I walk (4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **ii-V-I is the road home.** You open the door, feel the tension, and arrive.
- **V→I is the highlight.** Confirm the moment of resolution with your ear.
- **Minor takes a b3.** On Gm7 you step on the b3 instead of the 3rd.
