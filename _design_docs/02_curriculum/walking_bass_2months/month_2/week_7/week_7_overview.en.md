# Week 7 — Ornaments and Fills: Breathing Life into a Quarter-Note Line
> Curriculum: walking_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w7

Even a straight quarter-note walk comes alive with one <mark>ghost note ("chka")</mark> or eighth-note fill. A ghost note is like a small in-between step, so slipping a pitchless **dead note ("chka")** lightly onto a weak beat lets the line <mark>breathe</mark>. Now and then, spilling two notes with an **eighth-note fill** or connecting them with a **hammer-on** puts life into the step. What matters is the order — **keep the quarter-note skeleton** and lay ornaments on top of it. It's the same hand on **4-string** and **5-string**.

## This week's goal
By the end of this week, you can breathe life into the line with **ghost notes**, **eighth-note fills**, and **hammer-ons** without shaking the **quarter-note skeleton**. On the final day you complete an <mark>ornamented walk (F7)</mark> on 4- and 5-string.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Slip in a ghost note ("chka"). A ghost walk.
- **Day 2** — Eighth-note fill (two notes on a weak beat). An eighth-note fill.
- **Day 3** — Hammer-on ornament. Two flowing notes.
- **Day 4** — Complete the ornamented walk (4- and 5-string).

## Core feel
- **Skeleton first.** If the quarter notes wobble, the ornaments collapse too.
- **The ghost is an in-between step.** Pitchless and light, to give the groove breath.
- **Ornaments are seasoning.** Not too much — just enough for the line to breathe.
