# Week 8 — Chord-maker recording challenge: not a test, a stage
> Curriculum: chord_building_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w8

Over two months you learned a lot — 6th/5th/4th-string roots, shell voicings, 9th tensions, top-note leading. This week is **not a test, it's a stage.** Instead of memorizing one right answer, you **choose** the voicing that fits and leave your own comping on a recording. The backing is an R&B/jazz-flavored **ii–V–I loop (Dm7 → G7 → Cmaj7)** — over this four-bar loop, you "compose" a comp by mixing everything you've learned.

## This week's goal
Grab the same progression freely in several voicings (shell, 9-chords, high register, top notes), dress it with a rhythmic groove, add your own color with tensions and top notes, and finally record the whole thing and analyze it yourself. This is the crystallization of two months.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Challenge orientation. An 8-week recap map + the ii–V–I backing. "Choice cards" grabbing the same chord in several voicings.
- **Day 2** — Rhythm/groove challenge. Making groove by changing the comp rhythm on the same chords.
- **Day 3** — Your own arrangement. Reharmonize a plain progression with 9ths and a top-note melody.
- **Day 4** — Final recording & self-analysis. Record your finished comp and diagnose with a checklist. A two-month recap and a bridge to next month.

## Core feel
- **Choice, not a right answer.** You choose how to grab this chord.
- **Mix it all.** Shell, 9th, high register, top notes — as the moment calls for.
- **The recording is your teacher.** Listen and fix it yourself.
