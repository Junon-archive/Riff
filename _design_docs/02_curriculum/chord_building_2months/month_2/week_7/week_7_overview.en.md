# Week 7 — Real voice leading: make the top note sing
> Curriculum: chord_building_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w7

Amateurs change chords in "blocks." Pros are different — they **choose chords so the highest note (the top note) sings.** Even the same chord has several voicings depending on what you put on top (you saw this in Week 5's inversions). Pick the ones where the **top note moves smoothly, like steps,** and thread them through a progression, and chord comping suddenly becomes a "song." This week you train that **voice leading** for real.

## This week's goal
In a progression (e.g., C-Am-F-G), connect each chord's top note with **minimal movement** (staying put or a one-note step), so the top note becomes a single melody line across the progression. And the reverse: set a melody first, then put chords under it.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — The idea of voice leading and top-note melody. Contrast how one chord has several top notes. "I choose the top."
- **Day 2** — Connect top notes with minimal movement across C-Am-F-G. Common tones stay; only the top steps.
- **Day 3** — The reverse. Set a top-note melody first (e.g., C-B-A-G descending), then put supporting chords underneath.
- **Day 4** — Weekly integration. Comp like a pro, holding the top-note melody with voicings that even add Week 6's tensions.

## Core feel
- **Top note = melody.** Line up the top notes across a progression and a song should come out.
- **Minimal movement.** Keep common tones planted; the top moves one note at a time. Big jumps are amateur.
- The union of Week 5 (top-note idea) + Week 6 (tensions) — keep the color while making it sing.
