# Week 5 — Opening the high register with the 4th-string root (D-shape): the top note begins
> Curriculum: chord_building_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w5

Through months 1–2 the root came **down** from the 6th to the 5th string. This week you go the other way and move the root **up to the 4th string.** Now chords ring in the **high register** — on the thin strings (1·2·3·4). The bright, shimmering sound here is the raw material of pro comping. And in these high voicings you meet, for the first time, the idea of the **top note** (the highest-sounding note) — which becomes the core of the next few weeks (6 & 7).

## This week's goal
Learn how R·3·5 sit on the thin strings over a 4th-string root (the D-shape), firmly mute the 6th and 5th, and always know **which note is the top (R, 3, or 5).** The finish line is tuning your ear to the crisp sound of high-register triads.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Discover the 4th-string root. Dissect the D-shape where R·5·3(top) sit on thin strings. Mute the 6th & 5th.
- **Day 2** — 4th-string-root major/minor. 3 → b3 works in the high register too. This time **the 3rd is the top note,** so changing character changes the topmost sound.
- **Day 3** — A taste of inversions. For the same chord, the color changes by **which of R·3·5 you put on top.** Three voicings, changing the top note.
- **Day 4** — Weekly integration. Slide the high-register triad sideways to comp a bright progression, thin and pretty.

## Core feel
- **The root is on the 4th string. Kill the 6th & 5th firmly.**
- **The top note is the new star** — up high, the topmost note drives the chord's impression.
- Still **interval switches** — stack 3·5 over R, and one fret on the 3rd flips bright↔dark.
