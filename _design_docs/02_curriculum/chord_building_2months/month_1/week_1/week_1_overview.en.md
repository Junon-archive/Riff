# Week 1 — Dissecting the 6th-string root (E-form): change character with one finger
> Curriculum: chord_building_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

This week's stage is a single **E-form with its root on the 6th string.** Here you'll feel in your hands that a chord isn't a "memorized shape" but an **assembly of the 3rd, 5th, and b7th stacked over the root (R).** And with just one principle — **lower the 3rd a half step for minor, add the b7 for dominant 7** — you switch Maj → m → 7 → m7 over one root by moving a single finger.

## This week's goal
Fret R·3·5·b7 over the 6th-string root with your eyes closed, and switch Major ↔ minor ↔ 7 ↔ m7 by moving one finger. The finish line: describing a chord not as "which shape" but as "where is the 3rd right now, and did I lower it a half step?"

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Dissect the 6th-string root Major (E-form). Fully break down where R·3·5 sit, by interval.
- **Day 2** — Major → minor. Feel the moment "3 → b3" turns bright into sad with one half step.
- **Day 3** — Build the 7 (dominant). Absorb the "wants-to-resolve" tension that adding the b7 creates.
- **Day 4** — Complete m7 + integrate. Cycle all four characters (Maj/m/7/m7) over one root, changing only fingers.

## Core feel
- **The root is the 6th string.** It's the reference point for every decision.
- **The 3rd and 7th change the character.** You barely touch the 5th.
- A chord isn't memorized — it's **assembled.** This week is the starting line for that feel.
