# Month 1 — "Building" chords from 6th- and 5th-string roots
> Curriculum: chord_building_2months · Month 1

One sentence for the month: **a chord isn't a shape you memorize — it's an assembly game of stacking the 3rd, 5th, and 7th over a root.** You start on a 6th-string root (E-form), move down to a 5th-string root (A-form), then mix the two roots to connect a ii–V–I by the shortest path (voice leading), and finish with 3-string shell voicings that drop the 5th. The goal is to understand — in **intervals (scale degrees)** — how moving a single finger changes the chord's character (Maj / m / 7 / m7 / Maj7).

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Dissect the 6th-string root (E-form). Switch Major → minor → 7 → m7 with one finger.
- **Week 2** — Move down to the 5th-string root (A-form). Maj7 arrives; hear the half-step between 7(b7) and Maj7(7).
- **Week 3** — Mix 6th/5th roots to connect a ii–V–I almost in place (voice leading).
- **Week 4** — Drop the 5th: 3-string shell voicings for real comping, closing out the month.

## What changes by the end
When you meet a new chord, you can solve it yourself: "which string is the root, and where are the 3rd and 7th?" You leave shape-memorization behind and gain an interval-based eye for chords.
