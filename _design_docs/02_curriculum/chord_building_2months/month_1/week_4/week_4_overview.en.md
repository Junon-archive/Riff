# Week 4 — Chord diet: shell voicings with only root·3rd·7th
> Curriculum: chord_building_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w4

The month's last week. Until now you filled all six strings. This week you do the opposite — you **throw notes away.** What decides a chord's color is the **3rd and 7th.** The 5th can go without the chord collapsing. So you boldly drop the 5th (and the doubled root) and play **3-string "shell voicings" of just root·3rd·7th.** With only three fingers, transitions get faster and, in a band, the sound is actually cleaner (no clashing with bass and keys).

## This week's goal
Freely grab 6th-string-root and 5th-string-root shell voicings (Maj7 / 7 / m7), and reconnect Week 3's ii–V–I **entirely as shells.** The finish line: always knowing "the identity of the notes you kept" (which is the 3rd and which is the 7th).

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — What is a shell voicing? Why the 5th can go. Full 6-string chord vs 3-string shell, side by side.
- **Day 2** — 6th-string-root shell set (Gmaj7 / G7 / Gm7). Root on the 6th string, 3rd and 7th on the inner strings (4th & 3rd).
- **Day 3** — 5th-string-root shell set (Cmaj7 / C7 / Cm7). Root on the 5th string, 3rd and 7th on the 3rd & 2nd strings.
- **Day 4** — Final integration. Reconnect Week 3's ii–V–I (Dm7-G7-Cmaj7) all as shells, crossing 6th/5th roots for real comping. A month's reflection.

## Core feel
- **The 3rd & 7th are the color; the 5th is just a pillar.** Diet it away and the chord holds.
- **Three fingers = faster changes + a cleaner sound.** Thin, inside a band.
- Always know the **identity (3rd/7th)** of the notes you kept — intervals, not memorized shapes.
