# Day 4 — Complete! The b3→3 smear lick (Week 5 complete)

## ① Theory

The final day of Week 5. You complete the **smear** you built all week into a single **ornament lick**. <mark>Hammer-on from **b3 (F)** to **3 (F#)** to make it bleed, then climb through the **5th** and **b7** — a short lick.</mark>

The lick is four notes. It starts with the **b3→3** smear, passes the **5th (A)**, and flows up to the **b7 (C)**. <mark>One half step dyes the whole lick with neosoul color.</mark> The smear of the first two days and the vamp insert of day three all live in this one line.

Today's goal is to complete this **smear lick** clearly at **BPM 72**. <mark>Not speed — when the half step bleeds cleanly and the lick climbs smoothly, you've finished Week 5.</mark> First, learn where the lick travels.

## ③ Today's practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
At **BPM 60**, fret the lick's four notes (**b3·3·5·b7**) one at a time. Check the sound first, without the hammer-on. <mark>Hear first whether the four notes link clearly.</mark>

**10–20 min · Brain training (smear lick slowly)**
Now add the **b3→3** hammer-on with **one pick stroke** and climb through the other two notes. If the half step gets muddy, put a touch more left-hand force in.

**20–40 min · Real smear lick (BPM 72)**
Repeat the two bars below. Bar 1 is the smear lick; bar 2 lands on the **root (D)** and rests. <mark>Feel the flow of settling comfortably onto the root after the lick climbs.</mark>

Here are just the mistakes that show up most as you complete the smear lick.

▶ The green 3rd is the smear's landing; the 3rd-string 5th and b7 are the lick's climbing path.

- **The smear is muddy.** Don't lift the first finger; keep it pressed and put the hammer-on in firmly.
- **The climb rushes.** Don't rush the two notes after the smear; place them clearly on the beat.
- **The root landing floats.** Fret the final root briefly and leave a resonance to settle down comfortably.
- **You raise speed first.** Drop to BPM 60 and complete the lick with the half step bleeding cleanly first.
