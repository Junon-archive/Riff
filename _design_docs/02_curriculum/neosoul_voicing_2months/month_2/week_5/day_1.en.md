# Day 1 — First step of the b3→3 smear

## ① Theory

From today it's **Month 2**. Last month you learned to grip chords; from this week you learn to caress them. The first tool is the **smear**. <mark>The smear pushes a half step up from **b3** to **3**, letting the sound bleed like paint.</mark>

In D minor, **b3** is **F** (4th string **fret 3**) and **3** is **F#** (4th string **fret 4**). Strike F, then hit F# with the neighboring finger to sound it — that's a **hammer-on**. <mark>Not two pick strikes but **one pick stroke** while the half step glides up — that's the heart of the smear.</mark>

Today aim for one thing only — sounding **one clean smear**. Slowly at **BPM 72**, listen for **b3→3** bleeding smoothly. <mark>Speed doesn't matter at all. If the one half step links cleanly, today is a success.</mark> First, let your eyes learn where the hand sits.

## ③ Today's practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
At **BPM 60**, repeat just 4th string **fret 3→fret 4**. Press **F→F#** slowly with two left-hand fingers. <mark>With no hammer-on yet, fret each note one at a time and check the sound first.</mark>

**10–20 min · Brain training (hammer smear slowly)**
Now make the **b3→3** hammer-on with **one pick stroke**. If the sound cuts out, the left hand lacks force. Hit **right behind** the fret with your fingertip.

**20–40 min · Real smear (BPM 72)**
Repeat the two bars below. Each bar is **one smear**, a **5th** landing, and one beat of rest. <mark>Prepare the next smear during that one beat of rest and the flow won't break.</mark>

**40–50 min · Recording / self-feedback (recommended)**
Record ten smears and listen back. Count how many bring **F#** clearly to life.

**Done when:** You can sound one b3→3 hammer-on smear at BPM 72, clearly and without the sound cutting out.

## ④ Tips / Common mistakes

Here are just the mistakes that show up first as you make the smear.

```json
    { "string": 4, "fret": 3, "label": "b3", "role": "scale" },
    { "string": 4, "fret": 4, "label": "3", "role": "target", "highlight": true }
  ]}
}
```
▶ From 4th string fret 3 (b3) to fret 4 (green 3rd). This one half step is all of today.

- **The hammer-on is too quiet.** Hit right behind the fret, precisely and a touch harder.
- **You pick twice.** The smear is one pick stroke. The right hand strikes only the first note and stops.
- **F# is muddy.** Don't lift the first finger (F); keep it pressed and hit with the second finger so the note lives.
- **You raise speed first.** Drop to BPM 60 and build the sound of a cleanly linked half step first.
