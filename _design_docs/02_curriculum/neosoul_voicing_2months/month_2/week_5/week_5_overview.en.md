# Week 5 — Double-Stop Smears
> Curriculum: neosoul_voicing_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w5

The secret to neo-soul blurring like paint is the **smear** — pushing a half-step up **from b3 to 3**. On a **double-stop** (two notes held together, a 3rd or 6th), you **hammer-on** one finger and the space between chords smudges softly. More than notes landing dead-on, it's that slightly smeared, connected feel that is the heart of neo-soul.

## This week's goal
Hold a 3rd/6th double-stop and apply a smear with a b3→3 hammer-on, then slot <mark>a blurring smear lick</mark> between the vamp chords. When the two notes ring together and the half-step smears smoothly, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — b3→3 half-step hammer-on. Catch the smear feel on one note (F→F#).
- **Day 2** — Double-stop smear. Add the ornament to the 3rd/6th two notes.
- **Day 3** — Slot the ornament between chords. Insert the smear lick into the vamp.
- **Day 4** — b3→3 smear lick complete. With a blurring ornament (Week 5 done!).

## Core feels
- **The smear is b3→3.** Push the half-step up and connect as if smudging.
- **Two notes together.** A double-stop, so the whole harmony blurs.
- **Hammer-on softly.** Don't hit hard — nudge it up gently.
