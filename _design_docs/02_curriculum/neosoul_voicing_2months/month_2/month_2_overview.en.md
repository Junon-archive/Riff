# Month 2 — Ornament, Groove & Synthesis
> Curriculum: neosoul_voicing_2months · Month 2

This month in one sentence: **neo-soul is finished with ornament and feel.** Over the colored vamp you blur expression with **double-stop smears** (a b3→3 hammer-on) and **grace notes**, then lay a loose, laid-back groove with **laid-back swing 16ths** and **muted cutting**. In the final week you melt voicing, chromaticism, smear, laid-back, and chord melody into one progression to play and record <mark>a full neo-soul comping piece you play on your own</mark> as you graduate.

## This month's journey
- **Week 5** — Double-stop ornament. Blur the 3rd/6th double-stops with a b3→3 smear (hammer-on).
- **Week 6** — Grace notes & smears. Add expression with grace notes and pull-off descents.
- **Week 7** — Laid-back swing-16 groove & muted cutting. A 16th-note comp that lies back.
- **Week 8** — Synthesis graduation → play through and record a full neo-soul comp (neo-soul graduation).

## What changes by the end
With b3→3 smears and grace notes your chords start to 'talk,' and with laid-back swing 16ths you can pull out a sticky groove that lies back without rushing. You melt everything you've learned — voicing, tension, chromaticism, ornament, laid-back, and chord melody — into one progression to finish a full neo-soul comping piece on your own. Now you're ready to color any vamp you meet with neo-soul.
