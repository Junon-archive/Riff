# Week 7 — Laid-Back Swing-16 Groove
> Curriculum: neosoul_voicing_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w7

Neo-soul comping isn't in a hurry. It **lies loosely back** (laid-back). Over a 16th-note grid you slip a pitchless **muted cut ("chick")** between the chord stabs and place the beat **a touch behind**, and a sticky groove comes out. More than nailing the beat exactly, that ease of lying just a little late is the neo-soul feel.

## This week's goal
Mix muted cutting into a 16th-note comping grid and place the beat a bit behind with a laid-back feel to finish <mark>a laid-back Dm9 comp</mark>. When you feel the sticky, unhurried lie-back, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — 16th-note comp + muted cutting. Weave the chord and the "chick" cut.
- **Day 2** — Laid-back. The ease of lying just behind the beat.
- **Day 3** — Swing-16 feel. Roll the 16ths slightly for stickiness.
- **Day 4** — Laid-back Dm9 comp complete. With a lie-back groove (Week 7 done!).

## Core feels
- **Laid-back is lying behind.** Don't cling to the beat — hold back a touch.
- **Cut without pitch.** Lightly mute the strings for just a "chick."
- **No rushing.** The ease is what makes the groove sticky.
