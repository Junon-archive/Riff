# Day 4 — Complete! The laid-back Dm9 comping groove (Week 7 complete)

## ① Theory

The final day of Week 7. You complete the **16th cutting**, **laid-back**, and **swing 16** you built all week into one groove. <mark>It's the **laid-back Dm9 comping** — a sticky comping groove that sits behind the beat.</mark>

The finished piece rolls on one chord, **Dm9**. Fill the 16th grid with **muted cutting ("chick")** between stabs, and lay the whole thing **just behind the beat**. <mark>When the cutting fills the gaps and the laid-back lays the groove back, the neosoul comping is complete.</mark> This week's three pieces all live in this one bar.

Today's goal is to complete this groove sticky at **BPM 72, swing 16**. <mark>Not speed — when your hand sits back, the cutting lives, and the groove flows loose, you've finished Week 7.</mark> First, learn the **Dm9** hand shape by eye again.

## ③ Today's practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
At **BPM 60**, play the **Dm9** stabs cleanly on the beat. <mark>You have to carve the on-beat into your body before you can lay it back.</mark>

**10–20 min · Brain training (cutting slowly)**
Slot a **muted cut** between stabs, one at a time. Make the "chick" by loosening the left hand slightly, and lay it back bit by bit.

**20–40 min · Real laid-back comping (BPM 72)**
Loop the finished piece, the **laid-back Dm9 comping**, above. At the end, use the loop below to lay back and then rest one breath on the root. <mark>When the lay-fill-rest flow circles comfortably, the groove is complete.</mark>

- **The cutting disappears.** Focus only on the stabs and you miss the cuts, leaving the grid empty. Put a "chick" in every gap.
- **You collapse while laying back.** Laid-back is just behind. The next downbeat returns to place and keeps the groove.
- **The stab smears.** Cut the four notes short and loosen at once to prepare the next cut.
- **You raise speed first.** Drop to BPM 60, get the lay-back and cutting comfortable, then raise to 72.
