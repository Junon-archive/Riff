# Week 4 — Voice Leading & Top-Note Melody
> Curriculum: neosoul_voicing_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w4

Lay down a chord and move **the highest note (the top note)**, and the melody sings. There are two secrets: the chords **move as little as possible** (**voice leading** that keeps common tones), while your ear always follows **the top note**. This is the first step of chord melody — and the gateway that wraps up Month 1.

## This week's goal
Connect the chords smoothly with voice leading that keeps common tones, then move the top note as a melody to finish <mark>a top-note melody vamp</mark>. When the chords stay calm but the top note sings like a song, you've crossed the line (M1 graduation).

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Voice leading. Keep common tones between chords, minimal motion.
- **Day 2** — Top-note awareness. Find the highest note of each chord.
- **Day 3** — Top note as melody. Connect the top notes into a line.
- **Day 4** — Top-note melody vamp complete. Chords + melody (M1 graduation!).

## Core feels
- **Chords move as little as possible.** Keep common tones, move only what changes.
- **Your ear follows the top note.** The top note is the song's melody.
- **Melody comes first.** The chords are there to support that melody.
