# Week 1 — Core Grips: Dm9 & G13
> Curriculum: neosoul_voicing_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

Neo-soul chords aren't heavy, white-knuckle grips. They're a few notes laid softly in the **mid-range** — that's all. The secret is a **rootless feel**: **the bass takes the root** while your hand caresses only the 3rd, 7th, and tension. This week you carve the two grips of the signature vamp **Dm9→G13** into your hands.

## This week's goal
Get the two mid-range grips Dm9 (x5355x) and G13 (3x345x) into your hands and move smoothly across <mark>the Dm9→G13 vamp</mark> so every note rings clean. When the two grips switch without a wobble, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Dm9 grip. Carve the mid-range shape with a `fretboard_diagram`, clean sound.
- **Day 2** — G13 grip. Get the 6th-string-root 13th color into your hand.
- **Day 3** — Switching the two grips. Dm9↔G13 with minimal motion, smooth.
- **Day 4** — Dm9→G13 vamp complete. One chord per bar (Week 1 done!).

## Core feels
- **The bass takes the root.** Your hand only paints color (3rd, 7th, tension).
- **The mid-range is soft.** Don't slam the low strings — caress the middle.
- **Switching is minimal motion.** Keep common tones, move only what changes.
