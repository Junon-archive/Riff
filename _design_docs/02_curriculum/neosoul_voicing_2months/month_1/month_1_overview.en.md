# Month 1 — Voicing & Movement
> Curriculum: neosoul_voicing_2months · Month 1

This month in one sentence: **neo-soul begins with caressing the chord.** First you get **Dm9 and G13 mid-range voicings** into your hands to grip the signature vamp (a rootless feel where the bass takes the root), then you lay **tension** for color, **chromatic approach** for the slide, and **top-note melody** for song on top. You color one vamp four ways to play and record <mark>a colored Dm9→G13 vamp</mark> as you graduate the first month.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Core grips. Grip the signature vamp with Dm9 and G13 mid-range voicings.
- **Week 2** — Coloring with tension. Change the vamp's color with add9, 11, 13, and sus.
- **Week 3** — Chromatic approach. Slide into G13 from a half-step-above Ab13.
- **Week 4** — Voice leading & top-note melody → play through and record the colored vamp (M1 graduation).

## What changes by the end
The Dm9→G13 vamp settles into your hands, and you can shift its color with a single tension and slide down to land from a half-step above. You take the first step of chord melody — moving the top note like a song over the chords — and two plain chords start to glow with neo-soul. Next month you layer ornament and groove onto this vamp.
