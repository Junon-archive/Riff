# Month 3 — Modes, Expression & Composition (Graduation Semester)
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Parent: ../overview.md · Scope: Week 9~12

Just getting here is a big deal. In Month 1, you took over the pentatonic neck and got your hands on the blues. In Month 2, diatonic playing, chord-tone targeting, and ii-V-I guide tones all became second nature. Now it's the final month. We're calling it "Modes, Expression & Composition" — or, put another way, your **graduation semester**. On top of the "picking the right note" skills you've built, we're finally layering in **color (modes)** for the first time, learning **how to sing that color (expression)**, and putting it all together into **a song that's entirely yours (composition)**. The whole point of this curriculum — choosing your own notes and singing on the fretboard — comes together this month.

## What We're Building This Month
On top of the pentatonic neck mastery, blues, diatonic playing, and chord-tone targeting you built in Months 1 and 2, we layer in **modal color (Dorian ♮6, Mixolydian ♭7)**. You'll switch modes on the fly as the genre shifts, and turn scale-running into real music with space and phrasing. And at the very end, you'll bring it all together — **composing, recording, and giving yourself feedback on one original solo.**

## 4-Week Flow at a Glance
| Week | Focus | What You'll Be Able to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **W9** | Dorian mode — the major 6th ♮6 (F#) color | Bring out F# as color over Am7 and Am7–D7, and record an 8-bar improv landing on each chord's 3rd (C/F#) |
| **W10** | Mixolydian mode — ♭7 tension resolving to the major 3rd, plus bluesy phrasing | Resolve the blue note into the major 3rd over A7 and A7–D7, connect two shapes with a slide, and land on each chord's 3rd (C#/F#) |
| **W11** | Switching modes by genre + a mini-lesson on space and phrasing | Instantly pick the right mode for blues/funk/rock backing, and land on the 3rd with genre-specific phrasing that sings |
| **W12** | Composing, recording, and self-reviewing an original solo | Compose and record an 8-bar original solo where Dorian and Mixolydian coexist, then self-retake using four review criteria |

## How We'll Walk Through This Month
Month 2's chord-tone targeting and ii-V-I guide tones are this month's landing engine. Here, you layer "modal color" on top of that for the first time — filling in both wings with the sophisticated minor-side color (Dorian, W9) and the bluesy dominant-side color (Mixolydian, W10), switching live between the two in real genre contexts (W11) with expression layered on, and finally assembling it all into an original solo (W12). Learn the color, then learn how to sing it, then write your own song — that's exactly the order.

## By the End of This Month (Your Month-End / 3-Month Finish Line)
- Instantly reach for Dorian (♮6) over minor backing, or Mixolydian (♭7) plus the blue note over dominant/blues backing, and color it on the spot.
- Switch your mode and phrasing mood in real time as the genre changes (blues, funk, rock).
- Combine your "land on the chord's 3rd (guide tone) every time it changes" instinct with modal color.
- Phrase like music, not practice, using space, motifs, and call-and-response.
- Compose and record your own improvised solo over a backing track, then grade and retake it yourself.

## And Then, Graduation
Remember the day you started these three months? The one where your hands froze the second a backing track came on — where you'd copied plenty of YouTube solos but never once picked your own notes. The self-feedback and retake on the last day of W12 (Day 4) is the final period on this curriculum. When a plateaued player who used to just copy can build their own solo, take their playing apart by ear, and cut a better take — that's graduation. The moment all those scattered pieces come together into one song, you're already not the same person who started. Let's walk these last steps together.
