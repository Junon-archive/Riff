# Month 2 — From "Hands That Run Scales" to "Hands That Know the Chords and Talk Back"
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Parent: ../overview.md · Scope: Week 5–8

Having made it through Month 1, you can now connect the pentatonic box across the whole neck. But there's one thing still missing. When the chord changes underneath a backing track and your hand just keeps circling the same box, no matter how fast you play, it still sounds like "someone who doesn't know the chords." This month, we break through exactly that wall. We'll grow the pentatonic's 5 notes into 7, learn to read the same fretboard in two different keys, plant chord tones all across the neck, and finally, land precisely on each chord's 3rd every time the chord changes — turning "hands that run scales" into "hands that know the chords and talk back."

## This Month's Goal
We'll take the pentatonic box you locked down in Month 1 and **expand it into the full 7-note diatonic world**. First, add notes to build out A natural minor (W5), then get the feel for relative keys by relearning that same fretboard as C major (W6), then map chord tones (1-3-5) across the whole neck (W7), and finally, over a ii-V-I backing track, complete the feel of **landing right on the beat on each chord's 3rd (guide tone)** every time the chord changes (W8). The home base running through every one of these weeks is the same as it's been for three months straight: **landing on the 3rd**.

## Week Flow
| Week | Focus | What You'll Be Able to Do When It's Done |
|---|---|---|
| **Week 5** | Add the 2nd (B) and 6th (F) → A natural minor | Layer two new notes onto the pentatonic 5 as ornaments/passing tones, and jam over Am while always letting that color resolve to the 3rd (C) |
| **Week 6** | Relative keys: Am ↔ C major | Read the same fretboard in two keys just by shifting the center tone, landing on the bright 3rd E during C sections and switching to C during Am sections |
| **Week 7** | Map chord tones (1-3-5) across the whole neck | Find the Am (A-C-E) and C (C-E-G) arpeggio positions with your eyes closed, connecting the two chords' 3rds with the smallest possible move |
| **Week 8** | ii-V-I guide tones F→B→E | Finish a line that lands precisely on the 3rd on the downbeat of every chord change in the Dm7→G7→Cmaj7 progression |

## This Month's Flow, in One Line
In W5, you'll build bridges (B and F) across the gaps in the pentatonic to complete all 7 notes → in W6, you'll realize those 7 notes are actually the same material as C major, and handle two keys on one fretboard → in W7, you'll pull just the chord-tone skeleton out of that surface and map it across the whole neck → in W8, you'll lay that map over a real ii-V-I progression and nail down the timing of "when to land," too. Each week feeds directly into the material for the next, so all you have to do is take them in order.

## What You'll Be Able to Do (End-of-Month Milestone)
- Move freely between **A natural minor and C major (relative keys)** on the same fretboard, well beyond the pentatonic.
- Point to where the **chord tones (1-3-5)** of Am and C sit on the neck from a mental map, and connect their 3rds smoothly.
- Land **right on the beat** on the guide tones (F-B-E) of every chord in a **ii-V-I (Dm7→G7→Cmaj7)** backing track, improvising with the sound of someone who actually knows the changes.
- Slow your recordings down to check and fix your own landing timing, vibrato, and note endings.

## Connecting to Month 1 (before) and Month 3 (after)
- **Carried over from Month 1:** This month assumes the pentatonic neck command and the 3rd-interval / 4-note-sequence habits you built in M1. You'll layer added notes and chord tones on top to expand into the diatonic world, but the home-base engine — landing on the 3rd — carries straight through.
- **Leading into Month 3:** Once you've got W7's chord-tone mapping and W8's guide-tone line down, M3 layers **modal colors** on top — Dorian (natural 6) and Mixolydian (flat 7) in W9-W10 — then adds space and phrasing (W11) before you move on to writing and recording your own original solo (W12).
