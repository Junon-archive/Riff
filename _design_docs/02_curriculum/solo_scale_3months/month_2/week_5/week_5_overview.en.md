# Week 5 — Just Two More Notes on the Pentatonic, and Suddenly It Sounds Like a Song
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w5

For the whole last month, you worked hard dragging the pentatonic 5 notes (A-C-D-E-G) across the entire neck. But does this sound familiar? "I know the box inside out, but my solos keep coming out stiff, like climbing stairs." That stiffness comes from something built into the pentatonic itself: **wide-open gaps**. You have to leap three frets from A to C, and there's a bare stretch above E too. This week, we're building exactly two bridges across those gaps. Just two extra notes, and suddenly your solo starts flowing like an actual "song."

## What We're Doing This Week
We'll add the **2nd (B)** and **6th (F)** to A minor pentatonic (A-C-D-E-G) to complete the 7-note **A natural minor (A-B-C-D-E-F-G)**. The key thing is not to mistake these two new notes for the "main characters." B and F are strictly **ornaments and passing tones** — bridges connecting the skeleton notes. And all of this flow eventually gathers in one place: **C (♭3), the 3rd of Am**. Yes, that same home base we've been talking about since last month.

## Why We're Doing This Now
In Month 1, you already made the foundation rock-solid. Now it's time to add notes on top of that — but if you just jam new notes in without a plan, your hand slides right back into "run it up and down" mode. So on the first day of this week, before adding anything new, we'll chew through pentatonic Form 1 again using **3rd intervals**. Skipping every other note like that gets your brain remembering **the coordinates of each note**, not just finger order. Once that foundation is set again, we'll plant where the new B and F are actually headed (→ C, → E) — the destination first — into your hands and ears.

## This Week's Journey (Day 1–4)
- **Day 1 · Make the Familiar 5 Feel New** — Retrain pentatonic Form 1 using 3rd intervals (skipping every other note, like A-D, C-E) instead of running up and down. BPM 80, and yes, it'll feel frustrating — push through it. Whenever you stop, land on C (♭3) without exception. Today is all about locking in "the destination the added notes are headed for."
- **Day 2 · Build a Bridge Between A and C (the 2nd, B)** — Slot B into that wide-open gap between A and C. B lives in two spots: 6th string 7th fret and 1st string 7th fret. One rule only: **always let B flow into C.** Use A-B-C as a seed and roll it tightly in 16th-note 4-note sequences. If you park on B, it floats awkwardly over Am.
- **Day 3 · A Bridge That Flows Downward (the 6th, F)** — This time, attach F just a half step above E. F lives at 5th string 8th fret and 2nd string 6th fret. F gets pulled down to E like a magnet, so the **F→E half-step resolution** sounds lyrical and sticky. If B was a bridge going up, F is a bridge coming down — the direction flips.
- **Day 4 · Put It All Together and Actually Jam** — Combine the completed 7 notes into one singing scale and improvise over an Am backing track. Use B and F only as color, and always end your phrases with a **vibrato landing on C**. About 70% skeleton (pentatonic) and 30% color (B, F) is the sweet spot.

## This Week's Core Sense
The landing point never changes — it's still **C (♭3)**, the home base running through all three months. Don't forget: the two added notes aren't destinations, they're waypoints on the way to one. **B flows upward (A→B→C), F flows downward (F→E)**, giving your solo a breathing, rise-and-fall quality. And all week long, simple up-and-down runs are off-limits — approach everything with 3rd intervals and 4-note sequences only. That frustration you feel is the sign of a new circuit forming, so push through it.

## By the End of This Week
- Sweep freely through the Am pentatonic Form 1 in 3rd pairs, and stop precisely on C whenever you want.
- Find the 2nd (B) and 6th (F) on the neck with your eyes closed, and smoothly resolve those color notes upward (to C) and downward (to E) respectively.
- Use all 7 notes of A natural minor over an Am backing track, improvising a short 4-bar solo where every added note lands cleanly on the 3rd instead of floating loose.
- Bring your landed C to life with vibrato, and feel the first taste of "scale practice" turning into "a song."
