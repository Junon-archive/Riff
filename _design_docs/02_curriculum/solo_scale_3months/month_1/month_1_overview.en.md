# Month 1 — Owning the Pentatonic Neck → Blues
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Parent: ../overview.md · Scope: Week 0~4

One thing worth noticing: up to now, your solos have basically been "run up and down inside one pentatonic box" — full stop. Your hands move fine, but the second someone hits play on a backing track and says "go ahead, play whatever you want," you freeze solid. This month is where we break that wall for good. By the time these four weeks are up, no matter where your hand lands on the A minor pentatonic neck, you'll be building blues phrases that land — the instant the chord rings — exactly on the note that says "this is it." This is the month scale exercises finally become music.

## This Month's Goal
We build this step by step. First, onboarding (Week 0) unrolls the map of where this course is headed and sets up the routine and environment you'll run every day. Then we expand the Am pentatonic from **one box to all 5 boxes, across the whole neck.** And we're not just running up and down — we drop the up/down autopilot and chew through the fretboard with **3rd intervals and 16th-note sequences.** On top of that, we plant the heart of this whole curriculum: **landing on the 3rd (C, ♭3).** In the final week, we add the blue note **♭5** plus **bending and vibrato**, turning what used to be a flat scale into a blues phrase that actually sings.

## Why This Matters Right Now
The first real step into improvisation comes down to two things: "seeing the whole neck" and "choosing your ending note on purpose." Get stuck in one box, and your ideas run dry fast. Land wherever you happen to land, and no matter how fast you play, it sounds like noise. Month 1 solves both at once. We connect the whole neck into a single map so your hands have somewhere to go, and we drill "land on the chord's 3rd" into your body so every phrase gets an ending that actually clicks into place. This engine carries straight through to chord-tone targeting in Month 2 and modes/composition in Month 3 — nail it here, and the next two months get a lot easier.

## Weekly Flow
| Week | Focus | What You'll Be Able to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Week 0** | Onboarding, self-check, warm-up | Understand the course philosophy, build a daily warm-up routine, set up backing tracks/metronome/recording, lock in the R and ♭3 positions |
| **Week 1** | Box 1 + 3rd/16th-note sequences → first landing | Run Box 1 sequences in both directions, land deliberately on C/A over a backing track and finish with vibrato |
| **Week 2** | Expand into Boxes 2 & 3 + slide connections | Connect Boxes 1-2-3 with slides, improvise landing on the 3rd across boxes |
| **Week 3** | Boxes 4 & 5 + whole-neck visualization | Map all 5 boxes across the whole neck, land on the nearest C from anywhere |
| **Week 4** | Blues (♭5) + bending/vibrato + one-chord jam | ♭5 as a passing tone, accurate bending/vibrato, record a one-chord jam and self-review |

Each week builds directly on the last. Week 0 locks in your positions and routine, Week 1 chews through a single box until the sequence is second nature, Weeks 2-3 take that same weapon and widen the stage to the whole neck, and Week 4 layers blues color and expressive technique on top. Tempo climbs a little each week (starting around BPM 60, working up to 80-90), but one rule holds for all four weeks — **never practice at a speed you can't actually play.**

## What You'll Be Able to Do by Month's End
- Connect all 5 Am pentatonic boxes into one map across the whole neck, running through it in both directions with 3rd intervals and 16th-note sequences.
- Land deliberately on the nearest chord tone (especially ♭3 = C) at the end of a phrase, no matter where you are on the neck.
- Melt the blue note ♭5 in as a passing tone for that blues color, and make your landing notes sing with accurate bends (nailing the target pitch) and wrist vibrato.
- Keep improvising over a one-chord backing track without freezing up, then record yourself and coach your own playing on timing, landing, and space.

## Connecting to Next Month (Month 2)
If Month 1 is the skeleton — "minor pentatonic + landing on the ♭3" — Month 2 puts flesh on those bones. We add the 2nd and 6th to the pentatonic to expand into the **natural minor** (W5), move between relative keys Am↔C (W6), map chord tones 1-3-5 (W7), and build toward ii-V-I guide-tone lines (W8). The "landing on the 3rd" engine you drilled into your hands in Month 1 becomes the direct foundation for chord-tone targeting — finish this month strong, and next month will feel a lot lighter.
