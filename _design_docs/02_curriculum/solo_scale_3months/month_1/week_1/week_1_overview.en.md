# Week 1 — Burning In Pentatonic Box 1 + 3rd/16th-Note Sequences → First Landing
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

This is finally the week your hands go to work. If Week 0 was just looking at the map, this week you actually move into the map's first neighborhood — Am pentatonic Box 1 (5th fret) — and re-learn it not as "hand momentum" but as individual coordinates. Then, with 16th-note and 3rd-interval sequences, you chew through that box completely, until by the end you reach your first-ever "deliberate improv": landing your phrases **intentionally on C (♭3) and A (the root)** over a backing track. This week is the real starting line of the whole course.

## This Week's Goal
This week, we break the habit of just running Box 1 up and down — for good. Instead of up/down, you chew through the fretboard with 16th-note and 3rd-interval sequences, starting slow (BPM 60) and climbing step by step toward your own ceiling (aim for 78+), without ever losing note clarity. On top of that, the finish line for this week is landing every phrase ending on a chord tone (C or A), no exceptions, and finishing it off with vibrato.

## Why Now (Connecting to Last Week)
In Week 0, you refreshed Box 1's positions and roles and got just a "taste" of 3rd intervals. This week, we take that taste and turn it into real training. C (♭3) — which you only got a nodding acquaintance with in Week 0 — finally goes into action as your actual landing target. The first real step of improv isn't flashy speed — it's the feeling of "I get to choose the ending note." Plant that feeling firmly inside a single box, and it keeps working perfectly once we widen the neck starting next week.

## This Week's Journey
- **Day 1** — We burn Box 1 into your ears. No up/down runs — instead, play the **16th-note ascending sequence** deliberately at a slow BPM 60. The whole game is whether those four 16th notes stay separate and alive instead of blurring together. If they blur, it's fine to drop to 55. On top of that, we lock in the three C positions until you can find them with your eyes closed.
- **Day 2** — We get a real taste of the **3rd interval** — skipping one note (BPM 65). Focus on the skip pattern, emphasizing the landing points (C and A), and practice filling 8 bars over a backing track using nothing but 3rd jumps, finishing on C.
- **Day 3** — Raise the speed without losing note clarity. Start at BPM 65 and climb in steps of 5 — 70→75→80→85→90 — but only move up once you've cleared both up and down runs cleanly at each speed. The BPM right before your 16th notes start smearing together — that's your real level today. Nailing it clean at 78 is a lot stronger than forcing your way through 90.
- **Day 4** — This week's finish line. Over an Am one-chord backing track, land on the R/♭3 target and finish with a **slide → land → vibrato** combo (BPM 70-80). Vibrato isn't a finger tremor — it's your wrist rotating slightly, like turning a doorknob. This week, just nail one thing solidly: a "slow and wide" vibrato.

## Key Feel (Target Notes & Philosophy)
- **No more plain up/down repetition — 16th-note and 3rd-interval sequences instead.** You have to break the autopilot before improv can open up.
- **The ending note is always a chord tone (C or A).** Get that aching, bittersweet color of landing on ♭3 (C) into your body especially. Play slow, land on the 3rd, and it becomes a song.
- Speed isn't the goal — it's raw material. It's just a tool for building "the control to aim precisely at your target even in a fast run."

## What Changes When You're Done
- You can run Box 1 in both directions using 16th-note and 3rd-interval sequences, with no up/down autopilot left.
- You can clear the sequence with no smearing at your own ceiling BPM (aim for 78+).
- Over an Am backing track, you can land your phrases deliberately on C (♭3) or A four times or more, finishing landing notes with vibrato so they actually sing. Get this one feeling down, and you're ready to widen the neck.
