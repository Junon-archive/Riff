# Week 3 — Boxes 4 & 5 + Whole-Neck Visualization
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

This is the week you fit in the last puzzle piece. Once you fill in Boxes 4 and 5, all five boxes up to the 12th fret connect into **one single map.** Add Boxes 4 and 5, and the map comes full circle, meeting back up with Box 1 an octave higher — the loop closes. By the end of this week, you'll have fully escaped "Box 1 prison" and be using the entire neck as one connected space. No matter where your hand happens to be, the instant the chord changes, you'll be able to **land on the nearest 3rd (C).**

## This Week's Goal
First, burn Boxes 4 and 5 into your hands, and connect Box 3↔4↔5↔Box 1-an-octave-up to complete the full 5-box map loop. Second, run a whole-neck 3rd-interval sequence connecting all five boxes, with slides linking them with no breaks. And finally, this week's finish line is full-neck improv — landing on "the nearest C to wherever my hand is right now," no matter where you are.

## Why Now (Connecting to Last Week)
In Week 2, you connected Boxes 1-2-3 with slides. This week, add Boxes 4 and 5, and the 5-box map comes full circle, meeting Box 1 an octave up again. Here's why that matters: the most frustrating moment in improv is when your hand is already up the neck, and you find yourself dragging it all the way back down to Box 1 just to land. Once the whole neck reads as one single map, you don't need to double back — you just hit the nearest C to wherever your hand already is. This week also breaks the habit of only ever favoring one particular box.

## This Week's Journey
- **Day 1** — We move toward the 12th fret. Burn in Box 4 and connect it with a slide from Box 3↔4 (BPM 80). Land on C (♭3) via the Box 3→4 slide eight times, then go all the way to A↔C round trips with your eyes closed.
- **Day 2** — The last puzzle piece. Burn in Box 5 and connect it through Box 4↔5↔Box 1-an-octave-up, completing the full loop of the map (BPM 78-85). Feel the moment the circle closes, right there in your hands.
- **Day 3** — Five rooms become one hallway. This is the whole-neck 3rd-interval run connecting Boxes 1 through 5. It runs through three slides (4th string 7→10, 1st string 12→15, 3rd string 14→17), so it absolutely has to start slow. Very slowly at BPM 70, climbing in steps of 5 — 75→80 — once it smooths out. The moment a slide's arrival note goes fuzzy, that's your ceiling for today.
- **Day 4** — This week's finish line. Full-neck improv, landing on the nearest C no matter where you are (BPM 80-90). If you can hit all nine C positions across the whole neck in order, you've got it.

## Key Feel (Target Notes & Philosophy)
- **Even lines running across the whole neck get the same 3rd-interval and 16th-note treatment.** You digest position shifts as part of the sequence itself.
- **Aim for "the nearest 3rd."** Don't loop back to Box 1 out of habit — just hit the closest C to wherever your hand already is. This is the core feeling behind whole-neck freedom.
- Force yourself to use both the low and high neck positions so you break the habit of favoring one box. A whole-neck run is much harder than a single-box run even at the same BPM, so it's completely normal to drop back to 70 for the whole-neck run even if you nailed Box 5 alone at 90.

## What Changes When You're Done
- You can visualize all 5 boxes as one map and run the whole neck up and down with 3rd-interval sequences.
- You can instantly find a specific degree (R, C) at any point on the neck.
- Over an Am backing track, you can move freely across the full range and land on the nearest 3rd (C) to wherever your hand is. Your scale map is now complete. Next week, it's time to paint it with color.
