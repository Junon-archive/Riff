# Week 2 — Expanding into Boxes 2 & 3 + Slide Connections
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

Last week, your hands were locked inside a single box the whole time — that probably felt tight. This week, we let them loose up the fretboard. We plant Boxes 2 and 3, and build **a bridge called the slide** to connect box to box so you can cross smoothly between them. And after moving into the neighborhood next door, you still land your improv on that chord's 3rd (C). This is the week you take a step out of "Box 1 prison."

## This Week's Goal
First, burn the positions of Boxes 2 and 3 into your hands and build accurate "bridges" — slides connecting Box 1↔2 and Box 2↔3. Second, run through all three boxes like a single highway, up and down, using 3rd-interval sequences. And finally, this week's finish line is improvising across boxes and landing every phrase ending on the nearest 3rd (C). Your weapons — 3rds and 16th notes — stay exactly the same as last week. We're just widening the stage.

## Why Now (Connecting to Last Week)
In Week 1, you chewed through Box 1 completely with sequences and nailed landing on C. Now it's time to expand that feeling to "the neighborhood next door." The slide is the key here. No matter how many boxes you have, if you can't connect them smoothly, they're just islands floating on their own. The accuracy of that slide-bridge is what unlocks freedom across the whole neck later on, so this week we make sure those seams are rock solid.

## This Week's Journey
- **Day 1** — We move one spot up the fretboard. Burn in Box 2, and build the first slide bridge connecting Box 1↔2 (4th string, A→C) at BPM 70. Redraw the map of where everything sits, even in this new neighborhood.
- **Day 2** — On to the third house. Burn in Box 3, and build the second slide bridge connecting Box 2↔3 (2nd string, A→C) at BPM 72. Now you've got two bridges standing.
- **Day 3** — Thread all three houses into one line. Play the 3rd-interval sequence connecting Boxes 1-2-3, paying extra-careful attention right at the slide points. Warm up on the two bridges from the last two days first, then run the whole line very slowly (BPM 70), climbing in steps of 5 — 75→80 — once the seams get smooth. The moment a seam stumbles, that's your ceiling for today.
- **Day 4** — This week's finish line. Improvise over an Am backing track, moving across boxes and landing on 3rd targets (BPM 78-82). The lower and middle stretch of the entire neck is now your stage.

## Key Feel (Target Notes & Philosophy)
- **Even when moving between boxes, no up/down runs — keep the 3rd-interval and 16th-note approach.** Treat the move itself as part of the sequence, so "the act of shifting" becomes music in its own right.
- **Connect with slides.** The accuracy of that bridge (the connection point) is the key to freedom across the whole neck. Go slow, and land squarely on the arrival note.
- No matter which box you move to, end every phrase landing on that chord's 3rd (C) or root (A). The stage gets bigger, but the landing principle never changes.

## What Changes When You're Done
- You've burned in Boxes 1, 2, and 3 individually and can connect them smoothly with slides.
- You can run through all three boxes up and down like a single highway using 3rd-interval sequences.
- Over an Am backing track, you can improvise across boxes and land on the nearest 3rd (C) wherever your hand happens to be. Half the neck is already in your hands.
