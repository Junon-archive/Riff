# Week 0 — Onboarding & Warm-up (Calibration for Intermediate Players)
> Curriculum: solo_scale_3months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w0

This week, you won't learn a single new technique — and that's exactly what makes it so important. Before we start laying bricks, we're unrolling the blueprint of what this house will ultimately look like. This week you'll get a first look at where the next three months are headed and why we "land on the 3rd," set up the daily warm-up routine and backing-track/recording environment you'll run all month, and dip a toe into the improv mindset of "I get to choose my own notes" for the very first time.

## This Week's Goal
Just three things to cover. First, understand this course's engine in your head — "when the chord changes, land on that chord's 3rd." Second, get a daily warm-up routine (alternate picking + finger independence) locked into your hands. Third, bookmark a few backing tracks and set up your recording tool so you have an environment for "hearing your own sound." On top of that, just get the R (A) and ♭3 (C) positions in Am pentatonic Box 1 into your eyes, and Week 0 is a wrap.

## Why Now (Entry Point)
You're not a total beginner here — you're a **"sort-of-intermediate"** player: someone who's covered songs before but never actually improvised a solo. So we're skipping the basics entirely — no naming the six strings, no fingering fundamentals. There's no reason to keep holding onto stuff you already know. Instead, this week does just enough warm-up and self-check to get you straight onto the main track (3rd intervals → chord-tone targeting → modes → composition) and confirm exactly where you're starting from. Short, but precise footing.

## This Week's Journey
- **Day 1** — We unroll the whole course map. You plant the concept of "why the 3rd, of all things" (the root's just a name tag — what splits bright from dark is the 3rd) purely as an idea, with no actual training yet. Knowing that Am's 3rd is C (♭3) is the whole job for today. Your hands stick to just **one note — A, on the 1st string 5th fret** — and play with rhythm alone in a "one-note improv." With only one note available, rhythm and space start jumping out at your ears instead.
- **Day 2** — We build the shape of your daily warm-up. **Spider walk (chromatic 1-2-3-4)** wakes up alternate picking and finger independence (bump up by 4 BPM whenever you hit BPM 60 clean, no fumbles), and improv grows from one note to **two (A + C, ♭3).** Pause on C when the chord rings, and you'll catch a small preview of what "landing on the 3rd" feels like — that little "oh, that just clicked" moment.
- **Day 3** — We refresh the positions and roles of every note in Am pentatonic Box 1. Up/down runs are only allowed briefly, just to loosen your hands, before we move straight into your first real taste of **3rd intervals (skipping one note).** The point is opening your ears to what that skip sounds like.
- **Day 4** — We set the metronome discipline ("never practice at a speed you can't play"), search "Am backing track" and bookmark a few slow, bluesy ones. You set up your recording tool and learn to listen back to your own sound using three checkpoints — **timing, landing, and space** — wrapping up the week.

## Key Feel (Target Notes & Philosophy)
- **Running up and down isn't the goal — it's just warm-up.** We allow it briefly in Week 0 to loosen your hands, then move straight into 3rd sequences.
- **Am's 3rd = C (♭3).** We're not training the landing yet — just burning the position and sound into your eyes and ears as "the spot I'll be stepping on soon."
- **Keep your note count close to zero.** Playing with just one note, then two, builds an ear that hears rhythm, dynamics, and space before flashy fingerwork. You'll feel firsthand this week why the best players can move you with a single note.

## What Changes When You're Done
- Your daily warm-up routine feels natural in your hands, so any day from now on, five minutes is enough to wake them up.
- You can see the R (A) and ♭3 (C) positions in Am pentatonic Box 1 with your eyes closed.
- You've bookmarked a few backing tracks and know how to record yourself and coach your own sound using the three checkpoints: timing, landing, and space.
- Got all four? Then you're ready to launch into Month 1 (real improv territory). Don't worry if your self-check turned up a lot of X's — this whole course exists to turn exactly those X's into O's.
