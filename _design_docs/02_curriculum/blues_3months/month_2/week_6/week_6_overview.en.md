# Week 6 — Expressive Bending, Vibrato, Slides
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w6

Push the same note up with a **bend** and it cries. The voice of the blues comes not from precise notes but from this expression. This week you add **vibrato**, which shakes a note into song, and **slides**, which glide in and out — three expressive tools in your hands.

## This week's goal
Push the 4th (D) up a whole step to sound the 5th (E) exactly, then land on the root with vibrato to complete <mark>a bending lick that cries right to the target pitch</mark>. When your bend reaches the target and your vibrato is steady, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Bending. Push half and whole steps precisely to the target pitch.
- **Day 2** — Vibrato. Shake the note from the wrist, not the fingertip.
- **Day 3** — Slides. Glide in and glide out.
- **Day 4** — Expressive lick, combined. The whole-step bend lick complete (Week 6 done!).

## Core feels
- **Bend right to the target pitch.** A half-way bend isn't crying — it's out of tune.
- **Vibrato comes from the wrist.** A slight turn like a doorknob; slow and wide, just one.
- **Enter with a slide.** Don't just fret the note — glide in and expression appears.
