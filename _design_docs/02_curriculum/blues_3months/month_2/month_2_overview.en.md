# Month 2 — Expressive Lead: the Voice of the Blues
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 2

This month in one sentence: **the blues cries through bending.** You built the body of the rhythm — now for the voice. Add one **blue note (b5)** to the **minor pentatonic Box 1** (5th fret) to make the blues scale, and make notes cry with **bending, vibrato, and slides**. With call-and-response and space, you weave Box 1 licks to play and record <mark>your first solo over 12 bars</mark> and graduate the second month.

## This month's journey
- **Week 5** — Minor pentatonic Box 1 + blue note (b5). One teardrop turns the pentatonic into the blues scale.
- **Week 6** — Expressive bending (half/whole), vibrato, and slides. Three tools that make a note cry.
- **Week 7** — Blues phrasing. A question phrase, an answer phrase, and the space between.
- **Week 8** — Box 1 lick vocabulary → play and record a first solo over 12 bars (M2 graduation).

## What changes by the end
Box 1 and the blue note are in your hands, your bends reach the target pitch, and your vibrato is steady. Question-and-answer phrasing and space make your licks sound less like drills and more like speech, and you weave them into a first solo over the 12-bar. Next month you grow that solo into a mature one that responds to the chords.
