# Week 2 — The 12-Bar Form + Dominant 7ths
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

In the blues, **12 bars** make one lap. Three chords — **I(A7)-IV(D7)-V(E7)** — turn in fixed places, and because all three are **dominant 7ths**, they give off that grainy blues color. The surprise: just move your Week 1 boogie shape to D7 and E7 and the whole 12 bars is done.

## This week's goal
Move the same boogie shape onto the A7 (5th string), D7 (4th string), and E7 (6th string) roots to run <mark>one full 12-bar lap without stopping</mark>, quick-change included. When your hand finds each spot without hesitating on a chord change, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Dominant 7ths. The A7, D7, E7 chord shapes with a `fretboard_diagram`.
- **Day 2** — Move the boogie. The same shape onto the D7 (4th string) and E7 (6th string) roots.
- **Day 3** — Run the 12 bars. One lap including the quick-change (IV in bar 2).
- **Day 4** — Connect the 12-bar boogie. Complete it with the I→IV move (Week 2 done!).

## Core feels
- **All three chords are 7ths.** I, IV, and V are all dominant 7ths — that's the blues color.
- **Same shape, new spot.** The boogie form just slides a string up or down.
- **Don't panic at the quick-change.** Bar 2 briefly visits IV and comes back — it's just the convention.
