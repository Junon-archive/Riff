# Week 3 — Rhythm Variation: 9ths, Stop-Time, Dynamics
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

The same 12 bars transform with **rhythm variation**. A **9th chord** gives a jazzier, more polished color than the boogie, and **stop-time** (a hit, then silence) creates drama. This week you learn that space matters as much as sound, and that dynamics bring the groove to life.

## This week's goal
Learn stop-time — hitting an open A7 and carving silence with rests — to play <mark>one dramatic stop-time bar where the space lives</mark>. Swap in a 9th for color, and once loud-soft dynamics are in there too, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — 9th chord. The A9 shape and sound with a `fretboard_diagram`.
- **Day 2** — Stop-time. Hit, then carve silence with `rest`.
- **Day 3** — Dynamics. Push-pull loud and soft to bring the groove alive.
- **Day 4** — Variation, combined. The stop-time A7 stabs complete (Week 3 done!).

## Core feels
- **Space is sound too.** Silence makes the next hit land dramatically.
- **The 9th is a polished tension.** It gives a jazzier, more urban color than the boogie.
- **Dynamics make the groove.** Hit everything hard and it goes flat.
