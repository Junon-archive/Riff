# Week 1 — Shuffle Feel + the A7 Boogie Riff
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

The identity of the blues is the **shuffle**. Roll your eighth notes **long-short (da-ga-dum)** instead of evenly, and a plain rhythm suddenly becomes blues. This week you carve that shuffle feel into your body and lay an **A7 boogie riff** on top (root with the 5th, 6th, and b7 bouncing above).

## This week's goal
Over an open A (5th string), open and close your 4th-string double-stops 5th→6th→b7→6th, rolling <mark>a bouncy 2-bar A7 boogie</mark> with a steady shuffle feel. When both notes ring clean and the long-short is alive, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Shuffle feel. Catch the long-short (da-ga-dum) triplet feel on one note / open string.
- **Day 2** — Double-stop shapes. Carve root+5th and root+6th with a `fretboard_diagram`.
- **Day 3** — Boogie riff. Connect R-5-6-b7 in one bar.
- **Day 4** — A7 shuffle boogie complete. Two bars with the shuffle (Week 1 done!).

## Core feels
- **Shuffle is long-short.** First eighth long, second short. Picture a horse's hooves (da-ga-dum).
- **Hold the open A down.** Only the 4th-string finger opens and closes across 5/6/b7.
- **Groove over speed.** Slow but bouncy is already blues.
