# Month 1 — Rhythm: the Body of the Blues
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 1

This month in one sentence: **the blues begins with rhythm.** Before precise notes, you learn the body-moving **shuffle feel** (long-short, da-ga-dum) and lay an **A7 boogie riff** on top. Then you meet the stage of the **12-bar blues form** (A7-D7-E7), add expression with 9ths and stop-time, and tie it off with a turnaround to play and record <mark>one full 12-bar shuffle blues</mark> as you graduate the first month.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Shuffle feel + A7 boogie riff. Root+5/6/b7 double-stops over a long-short groove.
- **Week 2** — 12-bar form + dominant 7ths. Move the same boogie shape to A7, D7, E7.
- **Week 3** — Rhythm variation. Color with 9ths, breath with stop-time, push-pull with dynamics.
- **Week 4** — Turnaround + intro/ending → play through and record a 12-bar shuffle (M1 graduation).

## What changes by the end
The shuffle feel sinks in, and you can loop the A7-D7-E7 boogie over the 12-bar form without a break. With 9ths, stop-time, and turnarounds you give plain repetition expression and closure — a foundation for carrying a whole blues on rhythm guitar alone. Next month you lay the lead (solo) on top of this stage.
