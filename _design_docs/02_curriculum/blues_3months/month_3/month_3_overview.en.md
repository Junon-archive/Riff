# Month 3 — Depth & Integration: Speaking the Blues
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 3

This month in one sentence: **you speak the blues.** Now you respond to the chords instead of running scales. When a chord changes you **land** on that chord's tone, mix minor and major 3rds for the **'BB' color**, and weave **slides, bends, and vibrato** into one sentence to mature your licks. Finally you fill <mark>12 bars trading comping and soloing on your own</mark>, record a whole tune, and get sent off to the solo/scale journey next.

## This month's journey
- **Week 9** — Chord following. When the chord changes, land on that chord's root/3rd.
- **Week 10** — Major/minor 'BB' color. The b3→3 half step for a mature blues color.
- **Week 11** — Expanding lick vocabulary + maturing phrasing. Weave the tools, relax with laid-back time.
- **Week 12** — Rhythm-lead dialogue → final graduation and recording + a solo/scale send-off.

## What changes by the end
You move your landing note as chords change to sound like you're 'following the changes,' and the BB color and mature phrasing make your licks sound like your own voice. On your own you fill 12 bars with comping (the question) and soloing (the answer) to complete a whole blues. If you want to widen this expression across the whole fretboard and into modes, the solo/scale journey is waiting.
