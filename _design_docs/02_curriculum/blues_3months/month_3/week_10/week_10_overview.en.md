# Week 10 — Major/Minor 'BB' Color
> Curriculum: blues_3months · Month 3 · Parent: ../month_3_overview.md · dayKey: m3.w10

The blues scale uses the **minor 3rd (b3=C)**, but mix in a touch of the **major 3rd (3=C#)** and you get a mature color like B.B. King. That one **b3→3 half step** turns cloudy into clear — the grown-up secret of the blues.

## This week's goal
Push up a half step from b3 (C) to the major 3rd (C#) to make <mark>a matured BB color lick</mark> over A7. When you can fret b3 and 3 side by side and choose the 3rd that fits the chord, you've crossed the line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Major 3rd (C#) position. Fret b3 and 3 side by side.
- **Day 2** — The b3→3 half step. That half step is the BB color.
- **Day 3** — Choose the 3rd for the chord. C# over A7.
- **Day 4** — BB color, combined. The major/minor BB lick complete (Week 10 done!).

## Core feels
- **The b3→3 half step changes the color.** A slight move from cloudy minor to clear major.
- **Choose the 3rd that fits the chord.** Over A7, the major 3rd (C#) belongs.
- **Only a touch.** Linger on the major 3rd and the blues flavor disappears.
