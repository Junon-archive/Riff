# Week 1 — Slap's Two Sounds: Making Thumb and Pop for the First Time
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

There's no groove yet this week. Instead, you make <mark>slap's two sounds</mark>. You learn the **thumb** (boom!, a kick drum), where the side of your thumb strikes a low string and bounces off, and the **pop** (snap!, a snare), where a finger hooks under a high string and flicks up — separately. The thumb strikes near the bridge with a forearm rotation, and the pop lifts the string with your index or middle finger. By week's end, you alternate the two to make your <mark>first "slap sound" (T-P) ever</mark>. Whether it's a 4- or 5-string, the strings we use are the same, so any bass can follow right along.

## This week's goal
Ring the open E evenly with the thumb, cross the E↔A strings cleanly with the thumb, snap the open G with a pop, and on the final day alternate thumb (root E) and pop (octave E) crisply at 60 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Thumb motion and tone. Bounce the side of your thumb off a low string with forearm rotation for an even open E thumb.
- **Day 2** — Thumb string crossing (E·A) + muting the unplayed strings. Cross E↔A cleanly with the thumb.
- **Day 3** — Pop motion. Hook your index and middle finger under a high string and flick up for an open G pop.
- **Day 4** — Thumb meets pop. Finish a slow T-P alternation (thumb root + pop octave) on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **Forget speed.** The only measure is two clear sounds.
- **The thumb strikes and bounces off.** Don't let it rest on the string.
- **The pop hooks and flicks.** Lift the string with your finger — painlessly.
