# Month 1 — Slap's Two Sounds: Building a Groove from Thumb and Pop in 30 Days
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 1

This month in one line: **the goal of your first month isn't flashy tricks — it's getting slap's two sounds, imitating a drum kit with one hand.** After learning the **thumb** (a kick) that strikes and the **pop** (a snare) that snaps separately, you weave them into T-P alternation, then set your first slap groove with the **octave shape**. You fill the gaps with pitchless **ghosts** ("chka") to make the groove bounce, and finally move the root to finish and record an <mark>octave + ghost slap groove</mark>. You learn 4- and 5-string together, so any bass is fine — fifty minutes a day, and the measure isn't speed, it's <mark>a groove that moves your body on its own</mark>.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Slap's two sounds. Learn the thumb (kick) and pop (snare) apart, then land your first "slap sound" as T-P alternation by week's end.
- **Week 2** — Weave a groove. T-P coordination and the octave shape for your first slap groove.
- **Week 3** — Make it bounce with ghosts. Fill the gaps with "chka" and mute the strings you don't play — a groove with bounce.
- **Week 4** — Finish the groove + graduate. Move the root and record an octave + ghost groove.

## What changes by the end
Four weeks ago you couldn't make a single slap sound; now you drive a drum-like, bouncing groove from start to finish with one hand. Thumb, pop, ghost, and the octave slap line stay in your hands, and you reach the goal — <mark>a groove that funks and bounces</mark>. You're ready to move on to Month 2 (funk applications) with 16th-note syncopation, legato, and popping melody.
