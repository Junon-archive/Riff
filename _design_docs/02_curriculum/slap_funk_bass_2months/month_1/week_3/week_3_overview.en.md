# Week 3 — Make It Bounce with Ghosts: The "Chka" That Creates Bounce
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

The secret to slap that <mark>funks and bounces</mark> isn't notes — it's the **ghost ("chka")**. Slip a pitchless muted strike between thumb and pop and the groove starts to breathe — like a drummer's hi-hat filler. Laying the left hand lightly on the string to strike without pitch is the **dead note**, and at the same time you mute the strings you won't play with both hands to kill the noise. Once you own this <mark>bounce engine</mark>, Week 2's flat rough groove comes alive. By week's end you finish an octave groove with the gaps filled by ghosts. On 5-string, keep the low B muted.

## This week's goal
Strike one clean pitchless ghost ("chka") with the left hand laid lightly, connect a thumb-ghost-pop flow, mute the unplayed strings with both hands, and on the final day finish a bouncing octave groove with ghosts at 75 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — The dead note "chka". Lay the left hand lightly and strike without pitch — one clean ghost.
- **Day 2** — T-ghost-P combination. Make the thumb-chka-pop flow smooth.
- **Day 3** — Two-hand muting. Silence the unplayed strings + place the ghost exactly, with no noise.
- **Day 4** — Ghost groove. An octave groove with the gaps filled by ghosts, on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **The secret to bounce isn't notes — it's the ghost.** "Chka" breathes life into the groove.
- **Mute the strings you don't play.** Kill the noise with both hands.
- **The ghost is a hi-hat filler.** Fill the gaps to keep it rolling.
