# Week 4 — Finish Your First Slap Groove: Polish, Move, Record
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w4

At last you record <mark>your first slap groove</mark>. That groove got funkier over three weeks — rough groove → ghosts → polish — and this week you refine it by **balancing** thumb tone, pop, and ghost, then **move** the root (E→G) to make it feel like a tune. After a rehearsal you repeat without stopping, on the final day you record the octave + ghost groove as a <mark>finished piece</mark> and graduate Month 1. Three weeks ago you couldn't make a slap sound; now you're the one recording a groove. On 5-string, try a heavier version with the low B root.

## This week's goal
Balance thumb, pop, and ghost to steady a one-bar groove, move the root E→G to grow it to two bars, get through a full run-through without stopping, and on the final day record an octave + ghost slap groove as a finished piece at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Polish the groove. A steady one bar from the balance of thumb tone, pop, and ghost.
- **Day 2** — Root movement and dynamics. A two-bar groove with an E→G variation.
- **Day 3** — Rehearsal. A full run-through you repeat without stopping.
- **Day 4** — Graduation recording. Record the octave + ghost groove on 4- and 5-string + a preview of next month.

## Core feel
- **The balance of three sounds is the finish.** Thumb, pop, and ghost as equals.
- **Move on without stopping.** Don't break the flow even on a mistake.
- **Record the finished piece and keep it.** Next month: funk applications (syncopation, legato, popping).
