# Week 2 — Weave a Groove: T-P Coordination and the Octave Shape
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

If you made two sounds in Week 1, this week you weave them into a <mark>groove</mark>. The key is the **octave shape** — one hand shape where the thumb strikes the root on string 4 and the pop snaps the octave on string 2. This shape is <mark>eighty percent of a slap groove</mark>, so once it's etched into your hand, all that's left is to lay rhythm on top. First you match **T-P** evenly to the metronome, settle the octave shape, and place eighth- and sixteenth-note rhythms to make your first slap groove (a rough octave draft) by week's end. On 5-string, try a heavier alternative with the low B too.

## This week's goal
Match T-P as an even boom-snap in front of the metronome, move cleanly across the octave shape (thumb root on string 4 + pop octave on string 2, +2 frets), lay eighth- and sixteenth-note rhythms, and on the final day make a rough octave slap groove at 70 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Even T-P and timing. Boom-snap exactly to the metronome.
- **Day 2** — The octave shape. Thumb = root on string 4, pop = octave on string 2 (+2 frets), skipping a string.
- **Day 3** — T-P rhythm patterns. Place eighths and sixteenths for a T-P with rhythm.
- **Day 4** — Your first slap groove. Finish a rough octave groove on 4- and 5-string (gaps get ghosts in Week 3).

## Core feel
- **The octave shape is eighty percent of the groove.** Etch one shape into your hand.
- **Match thumb and pop in volume.** Boom and snap as equals.
- **Leave the gaps open.** They're where Week 3's ghosts go.
