# Week 7 — Popping Melody: Singing on the High Strings
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w7

If the thumb holds the low end, the <mark>pop</mark> sings up high. This week you make a short melody with **popping** — snapping the high strings — and get a taste of the **double-stop**, snapping two notes at once. You sing a line rising and falling b3-4-5 over the E minor pentatonic with pops, and at the end snap two notes D+G together so they ring <mark>like a chord</mark>. Rolling the thumb down-up for the **double thumb** is the next step, so this week you just peek at the principle. By week's end you finish a popping melody line. Even on 5-string, the high strings stay the same.

## This week's goal
Make a 3-4-note popping melody rising and falling b3-4-5 on the high strings, learn one double-stop snapping two notes at once, taste the principle of the double thumb, and on the final day finish a popping melody line at 75 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Popping melody. Move notes on the high strings for a 3-4-note pop melody.
- **Day 2** — Double-stop. Two notes popped at once (D+G) — one of them.
- **Day 3** — 〈Taste〉 Double thumb. Just experience the principle of thumb down-up (advanced).
- **Day 4** — Popping melody line. The b3-4-5 melody + a double-stop at the end, on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **The pop sings up high.** Snap the melody upward.
- **A double-stop is two notes at once.** Make it ring like a chord.
- **Just peek at the double thumb.** The principle only — no rush.
