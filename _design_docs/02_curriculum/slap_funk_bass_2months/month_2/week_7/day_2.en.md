# Day 2 — Double-Stop, Two Notes at Once

## ① Theory

Yesterday you snapped three notes on the high strings **one at a time**. Today you snap two notes <mark>at once</mark>. This is called the **double-stop**. Pop two strings together and one bass rings out <mark>like a chord</mark>. The melody suddenly sounds thick and full. If one note is a song, the double-stop is **a chord sung by two notes together**. It's not hard — you're just using a pop you already know on two strings at once.

Today's two notes are **open D on the D string (string 2)** and **open G on the G string (string 1)**. **D** is the **b7** of E minor, and **G** is the **b3**. Both are open strings, so the left hand is easy — today's star is the **right hand**. Snap both strings at exactly the same instant so the two notes ring as <mark>one mass</mark>.

The knack is using **two fingers**. Set the **index** lightly under the lower D string and the **middle** under the higher G string, then snap both fingers <mark>upward at the same time</mark>. If one note is loud and one is soft, it's not matched yet. At **BPM 75**, slowly, focus on whether the two notes snap together **evenly sized** with one "snap." If two fingers feel awkward, you can also hook both strings with the index alone and pull. Either way, the key is hooking both strings to the **same depth**.

Even on a **5-string**, the two notes are the same as on a 4-string. The pop comes from the two high strings, so just deaden the low **B** with the thumb. Once one **D+G double-stop** rings clearly today, the day after tomorrow you'll place this sound at the end of a pop melody line. **20–40 min · Real play**
Repeat the double-stop at **BPM 75**. If one note <mark>goes soft</mark>, drop the tempo and match the strength of the two fingers. Learn it on the 4-string, then confirm the same sound on the 5-string.

**40–50 min · Record/feedback**
Record 30 seconds and listen for <mark>whether the two notes ring together</mark>. Note today's comfortable BPM too.

**Done when:** you can snap a D+G double-stop at BPM 75 so the two notes ring together evenly sized.

▶ **5-string.** The notes and spots are the same as on a 4-string. Always keep the low B deadened with the thumb.

- **Only one note is loud.** If the index and middle differ in strength, one pokes out. Match the **two fingers to the same strength**.
- **One note is late.** If the two fingers misalign, it splits into "da-dak." Snap **at the same time** to make one mass.
- **The sound smears.** Hooking the strings too long smears them. **Hook short and release**.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Absorbed in the double-stop, B rings easily. Always keep B covered with the side of the thumb.
