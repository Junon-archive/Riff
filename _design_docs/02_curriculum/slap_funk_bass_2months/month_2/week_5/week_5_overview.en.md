# Week 5 — 16th-Note Syncopation: Rolling the Groove Forward
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w5

If the Month 1 groove was in eighths, funk is <mark>16th notes</mark>. A grid that splits each beat into four (1-e-&-a) is like a drummer's 16 hi-hat slots. Lay thumb, pop, and ghost right onto it, but push the accents back to **place them pushed (syncopation)** and the groove starts to <mark>roll forward</mark>. First you count the **16ths** while striking an even thumb, then place T, P, and ghost into the 16 slots, and push with syncopation to make a 16th-note syncopated slap groove by week's end. Start slow (BPM 80) and get accuracy first. On 5-string, the low B alternative is nice too.

## This week's goal
Strike an even thumb while counting the 16th grid (1-e-&-a), place T, P, and ghost exactly into the 16 slots, feel the syncopation that pushes accents back, and on the final day make a 16th-note syncopated slap groove at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — The 16th grid. Count 1-e-&-a and keep the thumb even in 16ths.
- **Day 2** — Placing the 16 slots. Lay T, P, and ghost exactly onto the 16 grid.
- **Day 3** — Syncopation. Push the accents back for a forward-rolling feel.
- **Day 4** — The 16th syncopated groove. A groove with a 16th push after the pop, on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **16ths are 16 hi-hat slots.** Feel each beat as four.
- **Syncopation is pushing back.** Push it and it rolls forward.
- **Slow first, accurate first.** Get the grid at BPM 80.
