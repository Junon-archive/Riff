# Week 6 — Slap + Legato: Melody from a Pentatonic Slap Lick
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w6

If slap is groove, this week you add <mark>melody</mark>. After a thumb strike, laying on a **hammer-on** — where the left hand slams the next fret — gives you two notes from one strike, and the **pull-off**, flicking the fretting finger off, is the reverse. Put this legato over the **E minor pentatonic** (E G A B D) and flat slap starts to sing. First you make the hammer-on and pull-off each clean, then combine them with the pentatonic positions to finish a <mark>pentatonic slap lick</mark> by week's end. On 5-string, we introduce an extended lick starting on the low B.

## This week's goal
Add a hammer-on onto a thumbed note for two notes, flick a clean pull-off, combine both over the E minor pentatonic, and on the final day finish a pentatonic slap lick at 75 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Hammer-on. After a thumb strike, slam the next fret with the left hand for two notes at once.
- **Day 2** — Pull-off. Flick the fretting finger off cleanly.
- **Day 3** — Slap + legato combined. A short lick in the E minor pentatonic position.
- **Day 4** — Pentatonic slap lick. An ascending E-G-A-B-D slap lick, on 4- and 5-string.

## Core feel
- **Two notes from one strike.** Hammer-on and pull-off make the melody.
- **The pentatonic is your map.** Play over E minor's five notes.
- **Keep legato clear.** The added note must ring too.
