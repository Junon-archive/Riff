# Month 2 — From Slap to Funk: Recording a Classic Funk Groove in 30 Days
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 2

This month in one line: **the goal of your second month is to grow the slap groove you built in Month 1 into real funk.** You split the once-eighth-note groove into a **16th-note** grid and roll it forward with syncopation (**pushing** accents back), then lay **hammer-ons and pull-offs** onto thumbed notes to add melody with a pentatonic slap lick. After making a singing line and double-stops with **popping** on the high strings, you finally melt everything into one and record a <mark>classic funk slap groove tune</mark>. You learn 4- and 5-string together, so any bass is fine — fifty minutes a day, and the measure isn't speed, it's <mark>the funk that moves on its own</mark>.

## This month's journey
- **Week 5** — 16th-note syncopation. Place T, P, and ghosts onto a grid that splits each beat into four, pushed.
- **Week 6** — Slap + legato. Add melody to a pentatonic slap lick with hammer-ons and pull-offs.
- **Week 7** — Popping melody. Snap the high strings for a melody line and double-stops, with a taste of double thumb.
- **Week 8** — Graduate. Gather every element into a classic funk slap groove tune and record it.

## What changes by the end
Eight weeks ago you couldn't make a slap sound; now you melt octave, ghost, syncopation, and popping into one groove and record a <mark>classic funk slap tune</mark> from start to finish. A two-month graduation piece stays in your hands, and you're ready to move on to the next track (shuffle & bounce, walking bass).
