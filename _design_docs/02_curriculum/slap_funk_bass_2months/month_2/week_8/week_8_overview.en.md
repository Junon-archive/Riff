# Week 8 — Graduation: Recording a Classic Funk Slap Groove
> Curriculum: slap_funk_bass_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w8

This is the <mark>graduation piece</mark> of eight weeks. Octave, ghost, syncopation, popping — you melt everything you gained over two months into one groove and finish and record a tune that smells of <mark>classic funk</mark>. The structure is a one-bar octave + ghost **groove**, capped by a b3-4-5 **popping fill** at the end of bar two. After a rehearsal you repeat without stopping, you leave a final recording and play it back to yourself from eight weeks ago. Then we send you off to the next track (shuffle & bounce, walking bass). On 5-string, a heavier version with the low B root is nice too.

## This week's goal
Combine groove and pop fill into one bar, grow it into a two-bar song structure of main + fill/variation, get through a full run-through without stopping, and on the final day record a classic funk slap groove tune as a finished piece at 85 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Combine the elements. Lay a pop fill onto the octave + ghost groove for one bar.
- **Day 2** — Song structure. A two-bar tune of a main groove + fill/variation.
- **Day 3** — Rehearsal. A full run-through you repeat without stopping.
- **Day 4** — Final graduation recording. Record the funk slap tune on 4- and 5-string + a send-off to the next track.

## Core feel
- **Melt what you learned into one.** Octave, ghost, syncopation, and popping in one groove.
- **Finish without stopping.** Keep the flow even on a mistake.
- **Record the graduation tune and keep it.** Next track: shuffle & bounce, walking bass.
