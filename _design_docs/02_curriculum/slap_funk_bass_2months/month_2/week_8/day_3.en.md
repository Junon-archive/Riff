# Day 3 — Rehearsal, Playing All the Way Through Without Stopping

## ① Theory

**Day three of Week 8** — rehearsal! Today you flow yesterday's **two-measure** song, repeating it <mark>without stopping</mark>. Eight weeks ago even one measure was a struggle, and now you spin the two measures **several cycles** at a time. With tomorrow's recording ahead, today you build **the strength to go all the way through**.

The heart of rehearsal is **not stopping**. Even if you miss a note, **don't stop the hand** — just move on to the <mark>next beat</mark>. On stage the song keeps flowing even through a slip. Today, join the two measures and go all the way through **four measures** (two cycles) in one pass. Mistakes are rehearsal's friends. As long as you keep the flow, small slips barely show, so flow the song to the end with an easy mind.

**BPM 85** is the goal. Start slow at first and watch whether the **second cycle** turns at <mark>the same size and the same tempo</mark>. Energy tends to sag or speed up on the second cycle, so push **at the same strength to the very end**. Once the full pass settles, stretch it to three cycles, four cycles. A pass that turns without stopping is exactly the confidence of the stage.

Even on a **5-string**, the hand shape is the same as on a 4-string. Keep the low **B** deadened with the thumb and mind only that **the flow doesn't break**. On a 5-string a <mark>heavier</mark> full pass rooted on the low B is nice, too. If you spin four measures without stopping today, tomorrow's recording gets much easier. - **You stop at mistakes.** Stopping the hand over one wrong note isn't a rehearsal. Just move to **the next beat**.
- **The second cycle speeds up.** It's easy to hurry on the now-familiar second cycle. Hold **the first cycle's tempo** to the end.
- **The second cycle weakens.** Energy tends to sag and the sound thins. Push **at the same strength to the last note**.
- **Neglecting low B (5-string).** Absorbed in the pass, B rings easily. Always keep B covered with the side of the thumb.
