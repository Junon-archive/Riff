# Week 3 — Power Chords: Rock from Two Fingers
> Curriculum: beginner_electric_1month · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

Past Week 1's mechanics and Week 2's riff, this is the week the <mark>'rock sound'</mark> finally arrives. A power chord isn't hard — it's a single brick built from two fingers. Ring only two notes and mute the rest, then slide the shape sideways to change chords. That's the whole thing. By the end, a palm-muted power-chord riff lives in your hands.

## This week's goal
Ring a power chord as two clean notes with no stray strings, slide the same shape sideways into other chords, build a rhythm with palm muting, and on the final day play the power-chord riff through at the target BPM.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Power-chord shape (6th- and 5th-string root) + muting unused strings.
- **Day 2** — Moving the shape. Connect frets to chord names.
- **Day 3** — Palm muting + downpicking rhythm.
- **Day 4** — Finish the power-chord riff.

## Core feel
- **Two notes only, kill the rest.** A power chord is just root + 5th.
- **Slide the shape and the chord changes.** Same shape, new position.
- **Drive it with downpicks.** Next songs to try: You Really Got Me / Iron Man.
