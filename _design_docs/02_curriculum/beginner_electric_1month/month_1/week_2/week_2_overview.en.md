# Week 2 — Two Hands as One: Complete Your First Riff
> Curriculum: beginner_electric_1month · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

If Week 1 woke each hand separately, this week you <mark>lock them together</mark> to play a real riff. You'll land the fretting and picking on a single point of the metronome, cross the strings, and build a low, growling groove with palm muting. By the end, one E-minor pentatonic single-note riff lives in your hands.

## This week's goal
Get both hands landing together on the metronome, keep the pick moving across string changes, build a groove with palm muting, and on the final day play the single-note riff all the way through at the target BPM.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Two-hand sync + finger independence (the 1-3-2-4 variation).
- **Day 2** — Crossing strings (inside/outside picking).
- **Day 3** — Palm-muted chug.
- **Day 4** — Finish the E-minor pentatonic riff.

## Core feel
- **The hands meet on one point.** Fret and pick land at the same instant.
- **Palm muting rests the edge of your hand on the bridge.** A low, growling sound.
- **The riff's character is one note that pops (the 4th).** Next songs to try: Seven Nation Army / Come As You Are.
