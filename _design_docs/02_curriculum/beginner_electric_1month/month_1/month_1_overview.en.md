# Month 1 — From Chromatics to Power Chords: 30 Days of Building Your Hands
> Curriculum: beginner_electric_1month · Month 1

This month in one line: **the goal of your first month isn't to play well — it's to build hands that make one clean sound.** You'll start with posture and pick grip, wake up your left hand with chromatics and your right hand with alternate picking, then lock them together for your first riff. Next, power chords put a 'rock sound' in your hands, and finally you'll gather everything into one finished song you record. Thirty minutes a day, and the measure is never speed — it's <mark>clean tone with no buzz</mark>.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Wake up each hand separately. Posture, pick, downpicking, chromatics, alternate picking.
- **Week 2** — Sync both hands. Complete your first single-note riff with palm muting.
- **Week 3** — Power chords. Root + 5th (two notes), moving the shape, palm-muted riff.
- **Week 4** — Finish a song + graduate. Link the parts into one recorded song, and open the next door with pentatonics.

## What changes by the end
Four weeks ago even one open-string note was a struggle; now you play a whole song start to finish. Chromatics, riffs, and power chords stay in your hands, and you're ready to move on to the next track (solo/scale, funk rhythm, or chord building).
