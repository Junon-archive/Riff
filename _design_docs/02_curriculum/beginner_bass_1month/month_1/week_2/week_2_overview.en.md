# Week 2 — Hands That Make Silence: Muting to Leave One Note
> Curriculum: beginner_bass_1month · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

Eighty percent of bass tone is <mark>silencing the strings that shouldn't ring</mark>. This week your left hand frets one clear note while the rest of the strings get put to rest by your hands, making <mark>silence</mark>. You find the root one-finger-per-fret, kill the unused strings, and move between two notes with no smear. On a 5-string there's one extra low B to rest, so muting matters a little more than on a 4-string.

## This week's goal
Fret cleanly at low frets, rest the unused strings so only one note rings, move roots E→A→G without breaks, and on the final day play an E-G-A-E muted root line at 70 on the metronome with no noise.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — One finger per fret + finding the root. Get a feel for note names on the E·A strings.
- **Day 2** — Muting (the core). Release the left hand to cut the sound; rest the low strings with the right thumb and fingers.
- **Day 3** — Move the root (E→A→G) with no breaks.
- **Day 4** — Muting + movement combined. Complete the E-G-A-E muted root line.

## Core feel
- **Silence is playing too.** The sound lives only when the wrong strings are quiet.
- **Your hand is a switch.** Rest the last note right before you play the next.
- **On a 5-string, keep covering the B.** The thumb always sits on the low string.
