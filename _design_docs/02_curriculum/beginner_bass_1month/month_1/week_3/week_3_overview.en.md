# Week 3 — One Body with the Drums: Roots on the Kick
> Curriculum: beginner_bass_1month · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

The bass isn't an instrument that stands out alone. A song gets solid when you become <mark>one body with the kick drum</mark>. This week you lay eighth-note roots right on the kick and make groove with the <mark>rest (space)</mark> in between. You count the subdivisions in your body and move roots over two chords without wavering, putting the feel of locking tight with the drums into your hands.

## This week's goal
Count eighth notes in your body and lay roots on the kick, play an even eighth-note root line over one chord, move between two chords smoothly over the drums, and on the final day play an E-A eighth-note root line (with a pocket) at 75 on the metronome without wavering.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Feel the subdivisions (counting eighths) + lay roots on the kick.
- **Day 2** — Eighth-note root line (one chord) + a rest in the middle to make a pocket.
- **Day 3** — Two-chord change over the drums. Follow the root as the chord shifts.
- **Day 4** — Complete the eighth-note root-line song (two chords).

## Core feel
- **Don't stand out alone.** Your hand lands the same instant as the kick.
- **The rest makes the groove.** The hand keeps moving, but that spot stays empty.
- **On a 5-string, keep resting the B.** Hold the muting even inside the groove.
