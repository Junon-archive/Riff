# Week 1 — Wake Up Both Hands: From Posture to Your First Root
> Curriculum: beginner_bass_1month · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

There's no song yet this week. Instead, you build the hands that make <mark>one clean low note</mark>. You start with posture and strap, ring the open strings evenly with right-hand two-finger technique (index-middle alternation), cross between strings, and press your first note with the left hand — <mark>with no buzz</mark>. No pick here — two fingers walking in turns is where our sound begins. Whether it's a 4- or 5-string, the strings we use are the same, so any bass can follow right along.

## This week's goal
Ring the open E evenly with two fingers, cross the E↔A strings smoothly, fret a note with no buzz, and on the final day play an E·A root pulse mixing open and fretted notes — clear, at 60–65 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Meet the bass. Posture, strap, tuning; 4- vs 5-string; even open E with two fingers.
- **Day 2** — Steady the alternation + string crossing (E↔A); settle the thumb.
- **Day 3** — Left hand's first fretting. Press just behind the fret for one clean note.
- **Day 4** — Both hands together. An E·A root pulse mixing open and fretted notes.

## Core feel
- **Forget speed.** The only measure is a clean low note with no buzz.
- **Two fingers walk in turns.** Keep index and middle equal in volume.
- **Sore hands are normal.** Calluses are the answer.
