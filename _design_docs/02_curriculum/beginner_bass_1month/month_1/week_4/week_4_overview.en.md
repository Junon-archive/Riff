# Week 4 — A Real Bassline: Graduation, and the Next Door
> Curriculum: beginner_bass_1month · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w4

At last, a <mark>real bassline</mark>. Add a fifth and an octave to the hand that only played roots, and the line starts to bounce. The progression is E–C–G–D — the <mark>same progression</mark> as the beginner electric graduation song, so you can even jam with a friend on guitar. On the final day you record the finished song to play back to the you of four weeks ago, and send yourself off toward the next journey.

## This week's goal
Add a fifth to the root, put spring in the line with the octave box, navigate the E-C-G-D four-chord progression by root, and on the final day record the R-5-8 bassline as a finished song at 80 on the metronome.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Add the fifth (root+fifth). R-5 over one chord.
- **Day 2** — Add the octave. R-5-8 with the octave box (root +2 strings +2 frets, skipping a string).
- **Day 3** — Navigate the E-C-G-D four-chord progression by root + don't stop.
- **Day 4** — Graduation. Record the R-5-8 finished song + a four-week growth recap + the next-track guide.

## Core feel
- **Add a fifth and octave and the line bounces.** Spring it with the octave box.
- **Keep going without stopping.** Don't break the flow even on a mistake.
- **Record the finished song and keep it.** Next tracks: shuffle & bounce, slap/funk, walking bass.
