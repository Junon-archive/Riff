# Month 1 — From Roots to Basslines: 30 Days of Building Your Hands
> Curriculum: beginner_bass_1month · Month 1

This month in one line: **the goal of your first month isn't to play flashy — it's to land one accurate root note that locks in with the drums.** You start by making a clean low note with posture and right-hand two-finger technique, then create silence by muting the strings that shouldn't ring, and set a groove by laying eighth-note roots on the drum's kick. Finally you add fifths and octaves to complete a real bassline and record it. You learn 4- and 5-string together, so any bass is fine — thirty minutes a day, and the measure is never speed, it's <mark>a clean low note with no buzz</mark>.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — Wake up both hands. Posture, two-finger fingerstyle, string crossing, your first fretted note.
- **Week 2** — Muting. Silence the strings that shouldn't ring; a two-note muted root line.
- **Week 3** — Lock with the drums. Feel subdivisions, lay roots on the kick, an eighth-note root line.
- **Week 4** — Finish a song + graduate. Add fifths and octaves for an R-5-8 bassline you record.

## What changes by the end
Four weeks ago even one open-string note was a struggle; now you hold down the backbone of a song from start to finish. Roots, muting, groove, and the R-5-8 line stay in your hands, and you reach the goal that mattered — <mark>not breaking up with your instrument</mark>. You're ready to move on to the next track (shuffle & bounce, slap/funk, or walking bass).
