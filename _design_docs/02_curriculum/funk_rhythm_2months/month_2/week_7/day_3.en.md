# Day 3 — Tilt the grid slightly and the rhythm bounces

## ① Theory

Until now we've played **<mark>straight</mark>** — placing notes exactly on the 16th grid. Today you taste the **<mark>shuffle (swing)</mark>** that tilts that grid slightly.

Shuffle rolls a pair of eighth notes not "evenly" but **<mark>"long-short."</mark>** Voice it: "daa-dm, daa-dm" — the first long, the second short. This turns a stiff, mechanical rhythm into a bouncing, swinging groove. That <mark>"sticky, swaying" feel</mark> of blues, soul, and funk comes exactly from shuffle. If straight is a square grid, shuffle is that grid tilted and lilting.

One thing to know: shuffle is hard to notate exactly (it's really a triplet feel). So the convention is to leave the notation straight and mark in the title and text to **<mark>"swing it (long-short)."</mark>** Today's example is the same — the notation is eighth notes, but actually roll it "daa-dm daa-dm."

Today, just burning <mark>the feel of "swaying"</mark> into your body is enough.

## ③ Today's practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
BPM 60–70. Roll "daa-dm daa-dm" with your voice and <mark>transfer it to your hands</mark>. Alternate with straight.

**10–20 min · Brain training (today's target = the swing feel)**
Voice straight "da-da-da-da" and shuffle "daa-dm daa-dm" alternately to <mark>burn the sway into your body</mark>.

**20–40 min · Real groove (Examples 1·2 / BPM 74)**
Repeat **Example 1** (straight) 4× at BPM 74 → then **Example 2** (shuffle). <mark>Rolling the same notes long-short</mark> is the key.

**40–50 min · Record & reflect (recommended)**
Record and check: <mark>does the shuffle sway long-short</mark> / does it differ from straight / <mark>did you not snap back to straight mid-way</mark>?

**Done when:** you can feel the sway difference between straight and shuffle, and roll eighth notes long-short for a shuffle feel.

## ④ Tips / common mistakes

- **Snapping back to straight.** Shuffle is awkward and reverts to the grid. Keep rolling "daa-dm" aloud.
- **Over-swinging.** Too much shuffle collapses the rhythm. A slight long-short is enough.
- **Counting by eye only.** Shuffle is the body, not the notation. It comes from ear and voice.
- **Speed first.** Fast before the sway sets in escapes to straight. Slow, swing it.
