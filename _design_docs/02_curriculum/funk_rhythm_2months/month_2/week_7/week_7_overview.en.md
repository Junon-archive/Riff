# Week 7 — Staccato and shuffle: expressing through note length and sway
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w7

If so far you've learned "what to play," this week is "how to play it." The same note changes completely by whether you **cut it short (staccato) or sustain it (legato)**, and **swaying the grid slightly (shuffle)** gives a bouncing swing. It's the week of sculpting note length and timing to give the rhythm inflection and expression.

## This week's goal
To mix staccato and legato freely for inflection, tell the sway of straight from shuffle by rolling eighths long-short, and hold a groove combining the two over a backing with real expression.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — What is staccato? The technique of cutting the ring. Staccato vs legato.
- **Day 2** — Cut and sustain. Design length within one bar for inflection.
- **Day 3** — Shuffle. Sway the straight grid into a "long-short" swing.
- **Day 4** — Integration. A groove mixing staccato + shuffle over a backing.

## Core feels
- **Length is expression.** Cut to tighten, sustain for space.
- **Shuffle is in the body.** Roll "daa-dm daa-dm" for the sway.
- **How you play.** The same note, made to speak through length and sway.
