# Week 6 — Single-note funk: drop the chord, draw with one string
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w6

Funk guitar isn't only chords. Like Nile Rodgers, you can pick out one string at a time to draw a bass-line-like single-note line. This week you learn the control to kill the other strings completely and ring one clearly, and to mix ghosts into that line for a full groove. The root E sits at the 5th string, 7th fret — the same spot as last week's E9.

## This week's goal
To play an E pentatonic single-note line on the 5th/4th strings clearly with no noise from the others, fill the gaps with ghosts, and complete and record your own riff that locks with the backing bass.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — What is single-note funk? A short line on the 5th string alone + a perfect mute.
- **Day 2** — Crossing two strings (5th↔4th). The mute stays even across the change.
- **Day 3** — Line + ghost. Fill the gaps between notes with "chucks" for a groove.
- **Day 4** — Integration. A one/two-string riff over a backing, locked with the bass.

## Core feels
- **One string clear, the rest silent.** A perfect mute makes the line live.
- **The mute stays across string changes.** Kill the string you just played quickly.
- **Few notes, thick groove.** Ghosts fill the gaps.
