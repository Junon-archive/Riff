# Month 2 — Color and expression: minimal voicings, single notes, staccato, and your own track
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 2

Month 1 gave you a right-hand rhythm engine (motor, ghosts, accents) and a first chord (E9). This month in one sentence: **paint "tone color and expression" onto the same rhythm.** Compress thick chords into thin three-string triads, drop even the chord to strike one string and draw a bass-line, and widen your expression with crisp-cutting staccato and shuffle. The final week isn't a test but a stage — over a bass-and-drums track you pour out everything from two months to record your own groove.

## This month's journey
- **Week 5** — Minimal three-string voicings. Crisp high-register cutting and the left-hand mute.
- **Week 6** — Single-note funk. Strike one string for a bass-line (the Nile Rodgers way).
- **Week 7** — Staccato and shuffle. A tight snap via note-length control, a swinging groove.
- **Week 8** — The recording challenge. 50 minutes of making and recording your own rhythm over a backing.

## What changes by the end
The same rhythm can take on entirely different colors through three-string cutting, single notes, staccato, and shuffle. Without leaning on chord progressions, you become a real funk rhythm guitarist who carries a tune on right-hand groove and expression alone — and you keep two months' fruit as a recording.
