# Week 5 — Minimal three-string voicings: crisp high-register cutting
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 2 · Parent: ../month_2_overview.md · dayKey: m2.w5

That shimmering funk cutting sound comes not from thick six-string chords but from thin high-register triads on the **1st, 2nd, and 3rd strings.** This week you grab that three-string shape and learn the cutting control to hit a narrow target accurately. The crux is the left-hand mute that keeps the lower strings (4/5/6) from leaking even as the right hand sweeps wide.

## This week's goal
To grab E major/minor three-string triads, cut only the top three crisply with the lower strings killed by the left mute, and complete a cutting groove over a backing by layering Month 1's ghosts and accents.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Why the high three strings. The E major triad shape + three-string aim.
- **Day 2** — Minor and inversions. Changing color with one finger.
- **Day 3** — The lower-string mute. Only three ring even sweeping wide.
- **Day 4** — Integration. Ghosts and accents on three-string cutting over a backing.

## Core feels
- **The shimmer is in the high three strings.** Low strings dull, high strings sparkle.
- **One finger changes the color.** One fret on the 2nd string, major↔minor.
- **The left-hand mute is the battle.** Right hand wide, sound only three strings.
