# Month 1 — The right-hand groove engine: motor, ghosts, accents, and a first one-chord jam
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 1

This month in one sentence: **funk is completed by right-hand rhythm, not by chords.** You start from a 16th motor that never stops, add "chucks" with left-hand ghost notes, place accents on just a spot or two to shape the groove, and finally mix all three over a single real chord (E9!) in a one-chord jam. It's the month you learn, in your hands, that the right hand alone can carry minutes of music without any fancy chord changes.

## This month's journey
- **Week 1** — The right-hand 16th motor. A hand that never stops via wrist snap, up/down balance, and empty picking.
- **Week 2** — Left-hand ghost notes. A relaxed "chuck" that contrasts with real notes for drum-like rhythm.
- **Week 3** — Accents. Stabbing just one or two spots over an even motor to bring the groove alive.
- **Week 4** — One-chord funk jam. Motor + ghosts + accents all laid over E9 and m7 shapes.

## What changes by the end
Without changing chords much, you can build a grooving pocket with the right hand alone. Funk's three staples — a hand that never stops, ghosts, and accents — enter your hands, giving a solid floor for next month's minimal voicings, single notes, and staccato.
