# Week 1 — The right-hand 16th-note motor: building a hand that never stops
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w1

This week's stage is **the right hand alone.** Eighty percent of funk is right-hand rhythm, and its beating heart is the 16th-note motor. The habit of a right hand that swings like a sewing machine — striking or not — is the whole of this week. The left hand only mutes (fingers resting on the strings); you focus on the *evenness of the swing*, not the sound.

## This week's goal
To hold the 16th motor for four continuous bars, muted, without the hand ever stopping. Matching down and up as even twins, and using empty picking (the hand swings even where you don't strike) to pick out only the "strike spots" — that's the finish line.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — What are 16ths? Splitting one beat into four, "1-e-&-a." Down/up, and the hand that never stops.
- **Day 2** — The wrist snap. From the wrist, not the arm. Relaxing via the muted "chika-chika" scratch.
- **Day 3** — Up/down balance + empty picking. Even where you don't strike, the hand keeps swinging.
- **Day 4** — Weekly integration. Four continuous bars + your first groove over a backing track.

## Core feels
- **The right hand is a motor.** Like a pendulum, it never stops.
- **The swing is in the wrist.** Arm fixed, wrist only. The more you relax, the faster you go.
- **The hand moves even on unstruck spots.** Empty picking locks the timing tight.
