# Week 4 — The E9 one-chord jam: funk that carries minutes on a single chord
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w4

Onto three weeks of right-hand motor, ghosts, and accents, this week you finally lay a **real chord.** You grab funk's emblem — **E9** (that shimmer the 9th gives) — and reproduce all three weeks of rhythm over this one chord. Funk isn't music that changes chords a lot — one chord (E9!) can carry minutes on ghosts and accents alone. The month's finish line is recording your own one-chord jam.

## This week's goal
To grab E9 (and Em7 for color) cleanly, lay the three layers — ghosts, real notes, accents — on top, and complete and record your own one-chord jam that rolls for a minute-plus over a backing without changing chords.

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — Grabbing the E9 shape. The funky color the 9th (yellow) gives + re-applying Week 1's motor.
- **Day 2** — E9 + ghosts. The contrast of the chord ringing then "chucking" out (applying Week 2).
- **Day 3** — E9 + accents + an Em7 intro. One chord, yet accents complete the groove.
- **Day 4** — The final one-chord jam. Mixing three layers, recorded over a backing. Month recap.

## Core feels
- **Funk is rhythm, not chords.** Minutes on a single chord.
- **The 9th makes the color.** E9's shimmer comes from the 9th (F#).
- **Keep the shape, only pressure on/off.** Make ta/chick/pop while holding the chord.
