# Week 2 — Left-hand ghost notes: the unplayed spots make the groove
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w2

Onto Week 1's right-hand motor, this week you add **left-hand percussion.** With the hand fully relaxed and merely resting on the strings, you get a pitchless "chuck" — the ghost note. Funk's groove comes from the contrast between a clear real note ("ta") and a dead ghost ("chick"). The right hand keeps swinging; the left presses and releases, drawing rhythm like a drum.

## This week's goal
From one hand position, to move freely between real ("ta") and ghost ("chick") using left-hand pressure alone, and to hold a groove of rationed real notes over a backing track so the "guitar sounds like a drum."

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — What are ghost notes? The relaxed "chuck," and hearing its contrast with real notes.
- **Day 2** — Ghosts only, continuous. Building an even "chick-chick" hi-hat carpet.
- **Day 3** — Real + ghost mix. Placing where "ta" goes (rationing the real notes).
- **Day 4** — Integration. An alternating real (snare) + ghost (hi-hat) groove over a backing.

## Core feels
- **The unplayed spots make the groove.** Ghosts ("chick") fill the grain of the rhythm.
- **Pressure on/off is everything.** Same spot, left hand pressing and releasing.
- **Real notes get more refined the more you ration them.** Just a spot or two "ta," the rest "chick."
