# Day 1 — Funk's grind comes from restraint

## ① Theory

That "grinding" funk feel doesn't come from hitting everything hard. It's the opposite. Most of it flows softly, and the groove comes alive **<mark>only where you stab one or two accents</mark>.** That stab is the **<mark>accent</mark>.**

An accent is a sharp jump in volume on one specific 16th over an even motor. Hit all sixteen hard and it's just noise; keep fifteen soft and let one "pop," and <mark>a center of gravity</mark> appears in the rhythm. It's exactly like putting weight on one footstep as you walk — a rhythm is born. **<mark>Contrast is the groove</mark>.**

Today, as a first step, put the accent on **<mark>the "&" of the beat</mark>** (the third piece of "1-e-&-a"). In the notation below, the green spot is the accent. Load weight onto that "&" and you get funk's signature pull, as if the beat leans slightly forward.

At first, trying to accent, the neighboring notes swell too — or nothing pops out at all. That's normal. Hitting one note hard while relaxing the rest takes a few days of control. Don't rush; today, just grab the single feel of "hard only here." The moment this one hit <mark>lines up later with the backing snare</mark>, your groove will suddenly spring to life. Today is your first taste of that one hit's thrill — feel the moment a rhythm leaps alive from a single hard stroke on just one spot.

## ③ Today's practice (50-minute routine)

**0–10 min · Warm-up**
BPM 60–70. Over even ghosts, drop a single accent "pop" anywhere to warm up <mark>dynamic control</mark>.

**10–20 min · Brain training (today's target = the '&' accent)**
Count "chick-chick-POP-chick," pinpointing the accent spot. Picture <mark>the weight landing on the "&."</mark>

**20–40 min · Real groove (Examples 1·2 / BPM 66)**
Repeat **Example 1** ('&' accent) 4× at BPM 66 → then **Example 2** (backbeat accent). The key: <mark>only the one green spot pops while the rest stay soft</mark>.

**40–50 min · Record & reflect (recommended)**
Record and check: <mark>does only the accent spot pop</mark> / <mark>does the rest flow softly</mark> / <mark>is the accent exactly on the beat</mark>?

**Done when:** over an even motor, you can place the accent precisely on the "&" of the beat so only that spot pops while the rest flow soft.

## ④ Tips / common mistakes

- **Everything swells.** If neighbors swell with the accent, the contrast dies. Only one note hard.
- **Accent doesn't pop.** The rest are too loud to let it stand out. Make the background softer.
- **Right hand stops.** Fixating on the accent breaks the motor. Keep swinging; only load and release force.
- **Greed for speed.** If the contrast isn't audible it's useless. Slow, only the green "pop."
