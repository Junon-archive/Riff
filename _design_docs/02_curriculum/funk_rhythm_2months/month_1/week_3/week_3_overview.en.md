# Week 3 — Accents: hard on one spot only, dynamics are the groove
> Curriculum: funk_rhythm_2months · Month 1 · Parent: ../month_1_overview.md · dayKey: m1.w3

Onto the even motor (Week 1) and ghosts (Week 2), this week you add **accents.** Funk's grind doesn't come from hitting everything hard — it comes alive when everything else flows soft and you stab just one or two accents. The contrast of loud and soft is the groove. Finally you roll three layers — ghost, real note, accent — with one hand, and lock the accent to the drum snare.

## This week's goal
Over an even motor, to place accents precisely on the 16ths you choose, build a three-layer contrast of ghost / real note / accent, and hold a groove with the accent locked to the backing snare (beats 2 and 4).

## This week's journey
- **Day 1** — What are accents? Building contrast by accenting the "&" of the beat.
- **Day 2** — Moving the accent. Shift it to e/a and the same pattern grooves completely differently.
- **Day 3** — Ghost + accent combined. Dead, normal, hard — a three-layer contrast.
- **Day 4** — Integration. A three-layer groove over a backing, accent locked to the snare.

## Core feels
- **Dynamics are the groove.** Not everything hard — just one spot "pop."
- **Position changes the color.** One slot of accent difference, a completely different feel.
- **Three layers, one hand.** Ghost (hi-hat), real note (kick), accent (snare).
